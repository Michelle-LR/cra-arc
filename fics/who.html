Who has reporting obligations

You may have a reporting obligation in Canada if you are a reporting Canadian financial institution for the purposes of Part XVIII (FATCA) or Part XIX (CRS). A reporting Canadian financial institution may include:

  • Banks
  • Credit unions
  • Specified life insurance companies
  • Private equity funds
  • Managed funds
  • Exchange traded funds
  • Brokers

To be a reporting Canadian financial institution you must be all of the following:

  • Located in Canada
  • A financial institution
  • A listed financial institution
  • Not exempted from reporting (classified as a non-reporting financial institution)

A financial institution is located in Canada if it is resident in Canada or if it is a branch located in Canada of a financial institution that is not resident in Canada. However, it excludes branches of Canadian financial institutions that are located outside Canada.

A financial institution includes:

  • A depository institution
  • A custodial institution
  • An investment entity
  • A specified insurance company

For a complete list of listed financial institutions and non-reporting financial institutions refer to:

  • Guidance on Canada-U.S. Enhanced Tax Information Exchange Agreement - Part XVIII of the Income Tax Act (FATCA)
  • Guidance on the Common Reporting Standard - Part XIX of the Income Tax Act (CRS)

Part XIX (CRS) has fewer exemptions for non-reporting financial institutions than Part XVIII (FATCA). Ensure you check the definition for both since you may be a reporting financial institution (RFI) under Part XIX (CRS) even if you’re not one under Part XVIII (FATCA).

What does a reporting Canadian financial institution need to do

As a reporting Canadian financial institution , you need to:

  1. Register with the IRS to obtain a Global Intermediary Identification Number (GIIN) for the purposes of Part XVIII
  2. Apply customer due diligence procedures, such asgetting a customer's self-certifications or documentary evidence, to review and identify reportable accounts
  3. Report customer accounts held or controlled by US citizens/US tax residents or other non-resident account holders that are reportable under Part XVIII and/or Part XIX
