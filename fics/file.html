How to file

The following information details how to complete and file a Part XVIII (FATCA) or Part XIX (CRS) information return.

Under Part XVIII and XIX of the Income Tax Act, Canadian financial institutions are required to:

  • Apply due diligence procedures to identify reportable accounts
  • Annually report to the CRA the information required on a Part XVIII and/or Part XIX Information Return.

Steps

  1. Register for a U.S. identifier (for Part XVIII only)

    Canadian financial institutions who have to report under Part XVIII must register with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). Once the IRS approves their registration it will issue a Global Intermediary Identification Number (GIIN). The GIIN is a 19-character identification string that you include on your Part XVIII information return.

    If you are a sponsor filing on behalf of a reporting financial institution you will have to have a separate GIIN that includes “SP” in the category code (for example 98Q96B.00000.SP.124).

    It may take up to 15 days for the GIIN to become valid for filling your returns with the CRA.

    Refer to:

    • IRS FATCA registration

  2. Register for a Business Number (BN) RZ program account (for Part XVIII & XIX)

    To file a Part XVIII or Part XIX information return, you will need a Business Number (BN) RZ program account. 

    If you already have a BN RZ account you can choose to use that account or you may also create a BN RZ account dedicated to Part XVIII and Part XIX information returns.

    Refer to:

    • How to register for a business number or a Canada Revenue Agency Program Account

    Once registered, you will need access to the CRA's sign-in services:

    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client (for authorized representatives)

    If the CRA finds errors or missing information that you need to amend, you will be contact at the email address you provided when you filed. The CRA will let you know that you can download your list of notification errors on the CRA sign-in service. It is important that the individual listed as the contact person has access to the CRA sign-in services or that they can contact the financial institution to advise someone who has access to the CRA sign-in services.

    Refer to:

    • CRA sign-in services

  3. File your return electronically

    You must file your Part XVIII and Part XIX information returns electronically using the CRA’s Internet file transfer (an XML file) or Web Forms applications.

    To find out more on these applications, refer to:

    • Filing Information Returns Electronically (T4/T5 and other types of returns)

    You will need a Web access code (WAC) to file your information returns over the Internet. This applies whether you file using internet file transfer (an XML file) or the Web Forms, unless you use these applications through My Business AccountorRepresent a Client.

    If you do not have aWACor you have misplaced it, go to Online Web access code or call the e-Services Helpdesk at1-800-959-5525.
