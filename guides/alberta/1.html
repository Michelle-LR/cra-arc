Alberta personal income tax

What's new

New rates

The personal income levels and most provincial non-refundable tax credit amounts have changed.

Contributions to nomination contestants made after March 30, 2022 will no longer qualify for the political contributions tax credit.

The following tax rates are used in the calculation of your Alberta tax on taxable income:

  • 10% on the portion of your taxable income that is $134,238 or less, plus
  • 12% on the portion of your taxable income that is more than $134,238 but not more than $161,086, plus
  • 13% on the portion of your taxable income that is more than $161,086 but not more than $214,781, plus
  • 14% on the portion of your taxable income that is more than $214,781 but not more than $322,171, plus
  • 15% on the portion of your taxable income that is more than $322,171

Alberta benefits for individuals and families 

Alberta child and family benefit

This benefit is a non-taxable amount paid to lower- and middle-income families with children under 18 years of age. This program is fully funded by the Province of Alberta. The annual amount is split into four payments and is paid separately from the Canada child benefit.

You do not need to apply for the Alberta child and family benefit. The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) will use the information from your Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application to determine if you are entitled to receive this benefit.
