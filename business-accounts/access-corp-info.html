What the CRA needs to update corporate information

Info

The Canadian Revenue Agency requires, for all types of businesses, the names and social insurance numbers (SIN) of:

  • owners
  • directors, or
  • partners

Only authorized individuals listed for a business number can access or make changes to the information. Consent is necessary for any modifications or additions of representatives.

The CRA generally only shares tax information about a corporation through:

  • My Business Account
  • Represent a client
  • Mail
  • Telephone

The following individuals can request tax information:

  • director(s) of a corporation
  • D0ated authority (level 3
  • third-party representatives authorized by a director or a delegated authority
  • legal representatives for the corporation (eg. bankruptcy trustee, court-appointed custodian)
• To avoid any delays or issues, we must have up-to-date information on directors.

Add or update corporation and director information

If a new corporation is created, or the structure or directors of an existing corporation changes, you need to inform the CRA by taking the following steps:

Register with the federal or provincial authority

Register and keep your corporation information up to date with the federal or provincial authority. For a new or successor corporation, the information you provide must include: • effective date of incorporation • address of corporation • names of directors Incorporating bodies that automatically share information with the CRA [H4] If you register with one of the following authorities, they will automatically share the information you give them with the CRA: • Alberta • British Columbia • Corporations Canada • Manitoba • New Brunswick • Nova Scotia • Ontario • Prince Edward Island • Saskatchewan For a territory or a province not listed above, contact the CRA(see Step 2). Provide documentation to the CRA, if applicable [H3] Send official documentation regarding your corporation and director information to the CRA using one of the following ways: • the submit document service in My Business Account or Represent a Client • mail – visit Find a CRA address

Provide director SIN to the CRA

You must provide a director’s SIN to access My Business Account This step is necessary for a director to access My Business Account and authorize a delegated authority or other third-party representative for online access to the business tax account using Represent a Client. If you have already provided a director’s social insurance number (SIN) as part of registration documentation, we’ll update the CRA’s records. If you did not previously provide a director’s SIN,you can provide it in one of the following ways:: • a representative may use the submit document service in Represent a Client to send a request signed by the director • a director can call Business Enquiries at 1-800-959-5525 • a director (or a representative) can fax a request signed by the director to their tax centre (PEI TC, 1-833-724-7237 or Sudbury TC 1-855-276-1529) • a director (or a representative) can mail a request signed by the director to their tax centre For tax centre addresses, please visit Find a CRA address.

Non-resident directors of Canadian corporations

Non-resident directors need to take the extra step of informing the CRA of their non-residency status. Incorporating authorities do not normally provide it as part of the information they send to the CRA. One director can attest the non-residency status of all of the directors. Any directors that cannot be confirmed by this director will need to attest to their own non-residency status. You can provide us with a director’s name, non-residency status, address, and phone number in one of the following ways: • a director can call Business Enquiries at 1-800-959-5525 • a director (or a representative) can fax a request signed by the director to their tax centre (PEI TC, 1-833-724-7237 or Sudbury TC, 1-855-276-1529)a director (or a representative) can mail a request signed by the director to their tax centre (Find a CRA address) • • Non-residents access to CRA’s portals • Non-residents do not have the same access to the CRA’s online portals due to security and authentication requirements. If all of a corporation’s directors are confirmed to be non-resident in the CRA’s records, the CRA will try to verify an authorization request by phone. Authorizing a delegated authority or other third-party representative [H3] Once a corporation provides a director’s SIN, that director will be able to: • access My Business Account • authorize a third-party to handle tax matters for the director For more information, please visit: • Representative authorization • Levels and scope of authorization that you can give representatives of your business The quickest way a director can authorize a representative is by entering their representative’s RepID, GroupID, or BN in the “Manage authorized representatives” section in My Business Account. Third-party representatives can also ask for authorization on behalf of directors through the: • Represent a Client portal • Business Consent Service in their certified tax software However, a director will still need to confirm these authorization requests in My Business Account. For more information on this two-step verification process, please visit Confirm my representative’s authorization request. Those corporations that are part of the CRA’s large business population may be able to reach out to a CRA large business audit case manager with whom they have an ongoing relationship to obtain guidance or support in relation to the Confirm my Representative process.
