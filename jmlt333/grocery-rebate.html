Grocery Rebate

As announced in Budget 2023, the Government of Canada introduced a Grocery Rebate to provide financial support to eligible Canadians.

One-time benefit

The Grocery Rebate was a one-time benefit that was:

  • issued on July 5, 2023
  • based on an individual's 2021 tax return information

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) will not be administering a similar Grocery Rebate in 2024.

Who received the one-time payment

You would have received the one-time Grocery Rebate if you were entitled to receive the GST/HST credit for January 2023.

To receive the Grocery Rebate, you had to file a tax return for 2021, even if you had no income to report for that year.

It is possible you may have been entitled to the Grocery Rebate, but not the July GST/HST credit or vice versa. The Grocery Rebate was calculated based on your 2021 tax return, but the next quarterly GST/HST credit payment in July 2023 was calculated based on your 2022 tax return. Please check your Notice of (Re)determination for more information.

Eligibility criteria

You and your spouse or common-law partner (if applicable) must have met the eligibility criteria for the 2021 base year to qualify for this rebate.

Payment details

The one-time Grocery Rebate would have been double the amount of your GST/HST credit payment from January 2023.  The amount was calculated based on your family situation in January 2023 and your 2021 adjusted family net income.

You could have received a maximum one-time payment of up to:

If you are single

  • $234 (no children)
  • $387 (with 1 child)
  • $467 (with 2 children)
  • $548 (with 3 children)
  • $628 (with 4 children)

If you are married or have a common-law partner

  • $306 (no children)
  • $387 (with 1 child)
  • $467 (with 2 children)
  • $548 (with 3 children)
  • $628 (with 4 children)

How your Grocery Rebate payment was calculated

If you received your GST/HST credit payment as a lump sum in July 2022, you still would have benefitted from the one-time Grocery Rebate.

Tax refunds, benefit and credit payments may have been applied to pay outstanding balances. You can check the status of your benefits by logging into your CRA My Account. Contact the CRA if debt repayment causes you financial hardship. Call 1-888-863-8662 for benefit debt or 1-888-863-8657 for tax return debt. Learn more at Canada.ca/balance-owing.

Payment Date

The one-time Grocery Rebate was issued on July 5, 2023.

If you did not receive the Grocery Rebate on the expected date, please wait at least 10 business days before you contact us.

If 10 business days have passed, you can go to our contact page , select Telephone numbers for personal taxes, benefits and trusts and go to the Get help with your CRA benefits and credits section for help. 

We also encourage you to review your Notice of Redetermination for more information about how much you are entitled to receive and when, and any changes to your payments.  If you are signed up for My Account, you can find your notice by clicking on the “Mail” link.
