[Managing your taxes when in an abusive or violent situation]

If you are facing an abusive or violet situation, including economic abuse, the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) is commited to helping you continue to get support.

You will never need to contact an abusive person or organization to provide any information to the CRA, use your My Account, or apply for and get your benefits.

Updating your personal information

To get your benefit and credit payments on time, in the correct amounts, your personal information needs to be up-to-date.

It is important to protect your personal and financial information. Regularly update your passwords to ensure your accounts stay secure.

  1. Cancel or authorize a representative

    Review who can access your CRA account and remove anyone who should not have access.

    If someone assisted you with your taxes in the past, they may have been granted authorization to access your account.

    Removing a representative online or by phone will immediately cancel their access to your account.

    You can also authorize a trusted person, such as a friend or family member, to be a representative.

    You can cancel or add a representative’s authorization:

    • Online using your CRA My Account
    • By phone or mail

    For more information, refer to: Authorize a representative

  2. Update personal information

    The CRA may need to contact other individuals or organizations to verify details such as your marital status or child custody information.

    Your personal information will never be disclosed to anyone who is not authorized on your account.

    Updating your address

    If you move, update your address with the CRA to keep getting your payments and any mail we may send to you, even if you use direct deposit and your bank account does not change.

    You can give us any temporary address until you have a permanent address available. This could include:

    • A friend's house

    • A shelter

    • A temporary safe place

    If you were using a joint email account, you may need to open a new account in your name.

    You should do this to maximize safety and if you choose to register for the CRA My Account.

    Updating your marital status

    If you separate from your spouse or common-law partner, you must wait 90 days before letting the CRA know.

    We will then recalculate your benefits based on your new marital status and adjusted family net income.

    The adjustment will take effect the month after your marital status changed.

    Updating your custody information

    To make sure you get your benefit and credit payments on time and in the correct amounts, the CRA needs to know if:

    • a child starts to live with you
    • a child is no longer in your care
    • a shared custody arrangement has changed

    If you are the primary caregiver for a child or children in your home but are not receiving the Canada child benefit (CCB), consider applying now.

    Apply for the Canada child benefit (CCB)

    Updating your direct deposit information

    For direct deposit payments, make sure the CRA has the correct financial account information on file.

    If you were using a joint account, you may need to open a new account in your name. It’s important to provide this new account information to us as soon as possible.

    Update your direct deposit information at least 4 weeks before your next benefit payment is issued to make sure it is deposited into your new account on time.

    Do not close your old account until your first payment has been deposited to your new account.

    Let us know if your benefit payments were deposited into a financial account under another person's name that you can no longer access. It may be possible to have the payment reissued to a new account or by cheque.

    Protecting your SIN

    Your social insurance number (SIN) is private and it is illegal for anyone else to use it.

    Learn how to protect your SIN

    If you need to apply for or retrieve your SIN

    Contact Service Canada to apply for or retrieve your SIN information if:

    • you don't have a SIN
    • you don't know your SIN
    • you don't have access to your SIN

    If Service Canada is unable to give you a permanent or temporary SIN, the CRA may give you a temporary tax number (TTN), which can be used to get benefit and credit payments, file your taxes, and sign up for CRA's My Account.

    Call Service Canada at 1-866-274-6627, or find a find a Service Canada office.

    You can update your personal information:

    • Online using your CRA My Account
    • By phone or mail

Getting your benefit and credit payments

Text

File your tax return every year

You don’t have to apply for benefits like the CCB every year, but the CRA uses information from your tax return to calculate the amount of money you receive. That’s why it’s important to do your taxes every year, even if you have no income or if your income is tax-exempt. You have until April 30 to do your taxes each year. This will help make sure you keep getting your benefit and credit payments.

Find out how to do your taxes or if you have a modest income, get help from a volunteer at a free tax clinic.

How to do your taxes

Free tax clinic

Apply for child benefits

If you care for a child under 18 and do not currently receive child benefits for them, consider applying now for the Canada child benefit (CCB). You can apply through your digital account with CRA called My Account or by filling out Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application.

Your spouse or common-law partner’s signature is not required on Form RC66 or any other benefit-related form.

Keep getting your payments

If you notice a change in your benefit or credit payments, find out why your payments may have changed or stopped. For example, if your spouse or common-law partner has not filed their taxes, your payments may be stopped.

If you have questions, or your benefit and credit payments have stopped, the CRA can help.

What to do when the CRA contacts you

You may get a letter from the CRA asking you to confirm your personal information or provide supporting documentation.

You can work with a trusted friend or organization to help you respond. Please send any supporting information back to the address listed on the letter or through My Account.

If your situation prevents you from getting the requested documents or information, you can send any of the following to explain your situation:

  • a letter from a trusted third party representative explaining your situation, for example:
    • a band council
    • a shelter
    • a resettlement office
    • a member of the clergy
  • a copy of a restraining order or an order of protection
  • a copy of a police report

If you have questions about the documents requested, or about your benefit and credit payments, please call 1-800-387-1193. The CRA will work with you to accommodate your or your family's needs.

Register for email notifications from the CRA. The CRA may send you notifications about your account by email. Make sure to provide your correct email address.
