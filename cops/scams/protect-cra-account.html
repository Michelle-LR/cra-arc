Monitor your CRA accounts regularly

It’s important to monitor your CRA accounts for any suspicious activity. Here are some things to look out for:

  • any signs of unusual behavior in your CRA accounts (My Account, My Business Account, or Represent a Client)
  • unexpected changes or changes you did not make, such as:
    • mailing address
    • bank or direct deposit details
    • representatives
    • benefit applications
  • your email address has not changed as the CRA sends notifications about changes to your account

How to protect your CRA accounts

Protect your CRA online account from incidents.

Regularly update user IDs and passwords

Change your login details and security questions periodically.

Use unique and strong passwords

Avoid using the same password for different accounts. Make them complex for added security.

Set up a PIN

Establish a Personal Identification Number (PIN) for your individual account. This helps confirm your identity in future communications with the CRA.

Keep personal and business info current

Ensure your contact details are up to date. The CRA may reach out to verify suspicious account activities.

Business owners should verify the applicable federal, provincial, or territorial corporate registry

Make sure the list of directors and contact information are correct.
