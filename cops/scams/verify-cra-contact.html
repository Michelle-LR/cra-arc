How to confirm it's the CRA calling

Always be cautious when you recieve a phone call from an unknown caller. When the CRA calls you, the employee will identify themselves by giving you their:

  • Name
  • Call back phone number

Never call the phone number back unless you have verified it yourself with the CRA.

Steps to verify it’s really the CRA calling

Intro line.

  1. Do not share your information

    Since you do not know if it's really the CRA calling you, do not provide any personal information to the caller.

  2. Get the caller's information

    Make sure you get the caller's:

    • Name
    • Office location
    • Call back phone number

  3. Tell the caller you will verify their information

    Let the caller know you will verify their information before calling them back.

  4. End the call

    You can use the verify the phone number tool to confirm the call is really from the CRA.

Before you call

To confirm your identity, you'll need your:

  • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • full name and date of birth
  • complete address
  • assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or be signed in to CRA My Account
If you're calling on behalf of someone else

If you are the friend or family member of someone who needs assistance applying over the phone and you are calling on their behalf, they must be present on the call to give their consent.

You will also be asked to verify your own identity when you call.

Reasons why the CRA may contact you

Here are some of the ways the CRA may contact you. Generally, the CRA may contact you by letter or phone, by email, or by automated courtesy calls.

Contact by letter or phone

The CRA may ask you for:

  • personal information to verify your identity, including your name, date of birth, or Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • details about your account or additional information, such as, receipts or documents
  • tax returns you have not filed
  • financial information, such as, your bank's name and location

If you have a debt with the CRA, we may:

  • ask you to pay any amount owing, using the CRA's payment options
  • notify you about possible legal actions to recover unpaid debts

The CRA notifies you when your:

  • tax return is being reviewed
  • recent GST/HST registration is being reviewed
  • GST/HST registration requirement is being reviewed
  • CRA user ID and password have been revoked
  • notice of assessment or reassessment is sent
  • an audit process is being initiated

The CRA may contact you to offer a service:

  • free tax help for your small business through the Liaison Officer service
  • support to help people access benefits and credits
Contact by email

The CRA will only send you email in 2 situations.

Notification of new mail from the CRA

You will receive an email notification from the CRA if you have new mail. Sign-in to your CRA online account to view it.

The CRA will:

  • send from: Canada Revenue Agency / Agence du Revenu du Canada
  • include subject line: "Not sure of text yet"

The CRA will not:

  • Ask you for personal or financial information
  • Request payment by prepaid credit cards or gift cards
  • Use aggressive language or tone
  • Threaten arrest or to send police

Request for a CRA link, form or publication

During a call or meeting with a CRA agent, they may be able to email you an link, form or publication.

The CRA will:

  • send from: CRA.DoNotReply-NePasRepondre.ARC@cra-arc.gc.ca
  • show the subject line: "Your CRA Links"

The CRA will not:

  • be from a specific user
  • contain any content besides CRA links
  • allow you to reply to the email
Contact by automated telephone message system

The CRA may call using an automated telephone message system as a courtesy reminder for:

  • income tax and benefit return filing
  • paying taxes by required quarterly instalments
  • filing and payment deadlines if you are a business with a new GST/HST account

The automated system only gives a message and does not include or ask for personal taxpayer information.
