How to confirm it's the CRA calling

If a CRA employee calls you, they will identify themselves by giving you their:

  • Name
  • Call back phone number

Be cautious of the phone number showing on your call display. It may not be a real CRA phone number. Never call back the number unless you have verified it yourself with the CRA.

Steps to verify it’s really the CRA calling:

  1. Do not provide any personal information to the caller
  2. Make note of their:
    • Name
    • Office location
    • Call back phone number
  3. Let them know you will verify their information
  4. End the call

Verify a CRA phone number

Type in the call back number you were given by the caller into the tool below. Click Verify number button to confirm the phone number is a legitimately from the CRA.

(e.g. 555-555-5555)

Before you call

To confirm your identity, you'll need your:

  • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • full name and date of birth
  • complete address
  • assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or be signed in to CRA My Account
If you're calling on behalf of someone else

If you are the friend or family member of someone who needs assistance applying over the phone and you are calling on their behalf, they must be present on the call to give their consent.

You will also be asked to verify your own identity when you call.

Reasons why the CRA may contact you

Here are some of the ways the CRA may contact you. Generally, the CRA may contact you by letter or phone.

Contact by letter or phone

To request information / Request for information

The CRA may need to ask you for:

  • details about your account or additional information (e.g., receipts, documents)
  • missing tax returns
  • financial information, such as, your bank's name and location

When you owe money to the CRA

If you have a balance owing, the CRA may:

  • ask you to pay the amount you owe by using CRA's payment options
  • notify by letter or phone about possible legal actions if your debt is not paid

Notify / Inform you of something

Intro line:

  • Notifying you of a review of your tax return
  • Informing you about the review of a recent GST/HST registration or your registration requirement
  • Notifying you if your CRA user ID and password are revoked
  • Sending a notice of assessment or reassessment
  • Initiating an audit process through a call or written communication

Offering a service

Intro line:

  • Offering free tax help for your small business through the Liaison Officer service
  • Providing support for helping your clients access benefits and credits

Contact by email

  • notify you by email when something is available for you to view in CRA's sign-in services
  • email you a CRA link, form, or publication when you request one during a call or a meeting with an agent
    • The e-mail will come from CRA.DoNotReply-NePasRepondre.ARC@cra-arc.gc.ca with the subject line "Your CRA Links."
    • The email will not:
      • come from a specific user;
      • have any content beside CRA links; or
      • invite any email reply.

Contact by automated courtesy calls

To remind you of income tax and benefit return filing, or those who may be required to pay their tax by quarterly instalments, we may send an automated telephone message.

Automated telephone messages from the CRA are courtesy reminders and will not include personal taxpayer information and will not ask you for personal information.

Businesses in Canada with a new GST/HST account may also get an automated message from us to remind them about filing deadlines and making payments.
