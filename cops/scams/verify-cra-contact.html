How to confirm it's the CRA calling

If a CRA employee calls you, they will identify themselves by giving you their:

  • Name
  • Call back phone number

When a CRA employee calls you, it will show as No Caller ID.

Steps to verify it’s really the CRA calling:

  1. Do not provide any personal information to the caller
  2. Make note of their:
    • Name
    • Office location
    • Call back phone number
  3. Let them know you will verify their information
  4. End the call
  5. Do not call the number shown in the caller ID or in your call log
  6. Use the [Verify a CRA phone number component]

Reasons why the CRA may contact you

There are many reasons the CRA could be contacting you and here are a few:

  • ask you for details about your account or to request more information (e.g. receipts, documents)
  • ask you for missing tax returns
  • call you or write to you to begin an audit process
  • ask you for financial information such as the name of your bank and its location
  • ask you to pay an amount you owe through any of the CRA's payment options
  • call you or write to you about legal actions the CRA may take to recover the money you owe, if you do not pay your debt
  • inform you that we are reviewing your return
  • inform you that we are reviewing a recent registration for GST/HST or your GST/HST registration requirement
  • to offer free tax help for your small business through our Liaison Officer service
  • to offer support in helping your clients access their benefits and credits
  • inform you if your CRA user ID and password have been revoked
  • to send you a notice of assessment or reassessment
