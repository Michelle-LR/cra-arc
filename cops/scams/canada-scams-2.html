Scams and fraud - Government of Canada

Learn the signs so you can protect yourself from scammers

How to recognize a scam

Many scams and frauds attempt to imitate government services in order to gain access to your personal and financial information. Know when to be suspicious.

The Government of Canada will not:

  • demand or pressure immediate payment by:
    • Interac e-transfer
    • Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin)
    • Prepaid credit cards
    • Gift cards from any type of retailer
  • threaten to arrest you, put you in prison, or deportation
  • use aggressive or threatening language
  • send you a link and ask you to click on it
  • ask or leave for personal or financial information

Notify the Canada Anti-fraud Centre

After you've reported a scam or any unauthorized changes to the appropriate government area, you should also notify the Canada Anti-fraud Centre. This information will help assist law enforcement with their investigations.
