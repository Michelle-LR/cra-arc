Scams and fraud - Government of Canada

Learn the signs so you can protect yourself from scammers

Know what to expect when we contact you

Scammers often contact you pretending to be from the Government of Canada. Know when to be suspicious.

The Government of Canada will not

  • demand or pressure immediate payment by:
    • Interac e-transfer
    • Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin)
    • Prepaid credit cards
    • Gift cards from any type of retailer
  • threaten to arrest you, put you in prison, or deportation
  • use aggressive or threatening language
  • set up an in-person meeting in a public location to collect a payment
  • charge a fee to speak with a call centre agent
  • send you a link and ask you to click on it
  • ask or leave for personal or financial information in a voicemail or email

Recognize and report a scam

Many scams and frauds attempt to imitate government services in order to gain access to your personal and financial information. Learn how to report and protect yourself depending on what government area was involved:

Service Canada

Learn how to identify Service Canada or 1 800 O-Canada fake calls, texts, emails or mail.

Canada Revenue Agency

Learn how to identify CRA fake calls, texts, emails or mail.

Immigration, Refugees, Citizenship Canada

Learn how to identify Immigration, Refugees, Citizenship Canada fake calls, texts, emails or mail.

Canada Border Services Agency

Learn how to identify Immigration, Refugees, Citizenship Canada fake calls, texts, emails or mail.

Canada Anti-fraud Centre

After you've reported a scam or any unauthorized changes to the appropriate area of the government, you should also notify the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre. This information will help assist law enforcement with their investigations.

  • Online reporting: Submit a report online

  • Call the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre toll free:

    • 1-888-495-8501
    • Monday – Friday 9AM – 4:45PM EST
