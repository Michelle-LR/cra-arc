What to expect when the CRA contacts you

Scammers often contact you pretending to be from the CRA. Know when to be suspicious.

The CRA will not

  • demand or pressure immediate payment by:
    • Interac e-transfer
    • Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin)
    • Prepaid credit cards
    • Gift cards from any type of retailer
  • threaten to arrest you, put you in prison, or deportation
  • use aggressive or threatening language
  • set up an in-person meeting in a public location to collect a payment
  • charge a fee to speak with a call centre agent
  • ask or leave for personal or financial information in a voicemail or email

Reasons why the CRA may contact you

Generally, the CRA may contact you by letter or phone, by email, or by automated courtesy calls.

If contacted by letter or phone

The CRA may ask you for information:

  • personal information to verify your identity, including your name, date of birth, or Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • details or additional information needed for your tax account, such as, receipts or supporting documents
  • tax returns you have not filed
  • financial information, such as, your bank's name and location

If you have a debt with the CRA, we may:

  • ask you to pay any amount owing, using the CRA's payment options
  • notify you about possible legal actions to recover unpaid debts

The CRA notifies you when your:

  • tax return is being reviewed
  • recent GST/HST registration is being reviewed
  • GST/HST registration requirement is being reviewed
  • CRA user ID and password have been revoked
  • notice of assessment or reassessment is sent
  • audit process is being initiated

The CRA may contact you to offer a service:

  • free tax help for your small business through the Liaison Officer service
  • support to help people access benefits and credits
If contacted by email

The CRA will only send you emails in 2 situations:

  • email notification of new mail within your CRA online accounts, which can be viewed when you sign-in
  • during a meeting or call with the CRA, they may send you an email you a link, form, or publication

Emails from the CRA will not be from a specific user, contant any links other than CRA, or allow you to reply.

Notification of new mail from the CRA

The CRA will:

  • send an email notification if you have new mail from the CRA, which can be viewed when you sign-in to your CRA online account

Send you a link, form, or publication during a call

The CRA will:

  • email you a link, form, or publication during a call or meeting with a CRA agent

The CRA will not:

  • be from a specific user
  • contain any content besides CRA links
  • allow you to reply to the email
If contacted by text or instant message

The CRA only sends text messages for multi-factor authentication for all of its sign-in services and if you enrolled with the telephone option.

The CRA will not use text messages or instant messaging such as Facebook Messenger or WhatsApp.

If contacted by automated telephone message system

The CRA may call using an automated telephone message system as a courtesy reminder for:

  • income tax and benefit return filing
  • paying taxes by required quarterly instalments
  • filing and payment deadlines if you are a business with a new GST/HST account

The automated system only gives a message and does not include or ask for personal taxpayer information.

Cryptocurrency scam (the CRA does not accept payment by cryptocurrency) Scammers targeting individuals by phone, requesting money be transferred via cryptocurrency to cancel an RCMP warrant for their arrest. The scammer then calls back, pretending to be the RCMP, and instructs the taxpayer to transfer money from their bank account to cancel the arrest warrant, promising to return the money once their name has been cleared. The scammer provides phone numbers and passwords to deposit money into a local coin machine or cryptocurrency terminal. Do not provide the individual with personal information or financial information. In addition:
  • Hang up if the caller requests money
  • Verify the caller is from the CRA
 Phone call phone-filter
Messages containing personal information Claims to be from the CRA and requests a payment or contains a link, as well as personal information such as a name, date of birth, or social insurance number. Do not reply to the text or click the link in the text message or provide them with your personal or financial information. Text text-filter
Canada Carbon Rebate scam New Messages come claiming to be the CRA Canada Carbon Rebate (formerly Climate action incentive payment). They contain a link to the Canada Carbon Rebate. Some include images taken from Government of Canada social media accounts. Do not click on the link or reply to the text message. Text text-filter
Emergency or disaster benefit scam Messages claim to be from the CRA emergency benefits and contain a link. They are being sent to those impacted by emergencies or disasters. Do not click the link or reply to the message. Text, Email text-filter, email-filter
