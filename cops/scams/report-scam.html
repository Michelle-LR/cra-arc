Report a scam or identity theft – Scams and fraud - CRA

If you're a victim of a scam or there is suspicious activity on your account, you should report it.

On this page

  • Report the incident to the CRA
  • Report misuse of your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • Inform other authorities about the incident
  • Notify the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre of a scam

Report the incident to the CRA

Contact the CRA to report a scam, suspicious activity, or suspected identity theft. These could include:

  • You suspect your CRA account has been compromised
  • You notice changes made to your CRA online account (banking, address, business, or personal information)
  • You notice a benefit application made without your knowledge
  • You want to disable or enable online access to your information in your CRA accounts

For details: Social Insurance Number (SIN)

Inform other authorities about the incident

Depending on what information has been taken or mistakenly given to a scammer, you need to report the incident to the proper authorities and organizations.

For example, if your personal or financial information has been compromised, contact each of the following:

  • Your local police
  • Your financial institution
  • Credit reporting agencies

Notify the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre of a scam

After reporting the scam with the CRA, you should also report the incident to the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre.

If someone attempted to scam you, but you did not give them any of your information, you should still report it.
