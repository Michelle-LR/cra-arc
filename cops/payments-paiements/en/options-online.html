Additional details for an electronic banking payment

Most financial institutions also let you set up a payment to be made on a future date.

Steps to take

  1. Sign in to your financial institution's online banking services for individual or business
For individuals
  1. Under "Add a payee" look for an option such as:
    • CRA (revenue) – Canada emergency benefit repayment or CEB repayment
      • Use this option to repay a CRA administered COVID-19 benefit
    • CRA (revenue) – current-year tax return
      • Use this option to make a payment for your current tax return. You can use this option only once per return
    • CRA (revenue) – tax amount owing
      • Use this option to pay any amount owing.
    • CRA (revenue) – tax instalment
      • Use this option to make payments toward the future tax year
  2. Enter your 9-digit social insurance number as your CRA account number
For businesses
  1. Under "Add a payee" look for an option such as:
    • Federal – Corporation Tax Payments – TXINS
    • Federal – GST/HST Payment – GST-P (GST-P)
    • Federal Payroll Deductions – Regular/Quarterly – EMPTX – (PD7A)
    • Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 1 – EMPTX – (PD7A)
    • Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 2 – EMPTX – (PD7A)
  2. Enter your 15-digit business number as your CRA account number.

Make sure the social insurance or business number is accurate so that the CRA can apply your payment correctly.

Additional details for a debit payment

My Payment is an electronic service that lets you make payments directly to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) using your bank access card. Pre-authorized debit (PAD) is a secure, online self-service payment option for you to pay your personal or business taxes. You can also use a third party provider to pay with Interac e-transfer.
