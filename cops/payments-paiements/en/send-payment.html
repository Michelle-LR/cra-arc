The CRA only accepts payment for defaulted federal student/apprentice loans or allowance overpayments that are in collections, on behalf of Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC).

The CRA only accepts payment for overpayment of benefits and assistance that are in collections, on behalf of Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC).

The CRA only accepts payment for custom import debts that are in collections, on behalf of Canada Border Service Agency (CBSA).

The CRA only accepts re-payment for a COVID-19 benefit that was issued by us, or that is in collections. Any payment issued by Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC), must be re-payed to ESDC.

Any non-resident requesting to pay for a security deposit by a bond must be approved by the Minister. It must be payed through a third party bonding company, along with submitting Form GST 114.

Review the form of security for details about acceptable bonds.

Mail your payment to the CRA by following these steps:

  • write the account number shown on the remittance form on the front of your cheque or money order
  • make your cheque or money order payable to the Receiver General for Canada
  • mail the remittance form, with your cheque or money order, to the CRA at the address on the back of the remittance form
  • keep a record of the amount and the date of your payment

Electronic banking (including pre-authorized)
Description

If you file your GST/HST return electronically using GST/HST NETFILE or GST/HST TELEFILE, you can still remit your GST/HST at your financial institution. Use Form RC158, Remittance Voucher – Payment on Filing to pay the amount owing. Do not use the remittance part of your GST/HST return.

Other remittance forms for GST/HST payments include:

  • Form RC159, Remittance Voucher – Amount Owing, to remit an amount owing or an outstanding balance for a period that has already been assessed
  • Form RC160, Remittance Voucher – Interim Payments, to remit your instalment payments
  • Form RC177, Remittance Voucher – Balance Due, to remit an amount owing before you file your return

Steps on how to avoid CRA scams

Paying instalments by mail or in person

You will need your instalment remittance voucher (form INNS3) to ensure your payment is applied to the correct account.

Your remittance voucher is included in your instalment reminder package the CRA mails to you, unless you pay instalments by pre-authorized debit.

Get a remittance voucher:

  • log in to CRA My Account or Represent a client
  • download a fillable remittance voucher
  • call our automated TIPS line at 1-800-267-6999 (opens up phone application)1-800-267-6999
