What is service feedback

Service feedback can be a service complaint, suggestion, or compliment about a service experience you had with the CRA. By submitting feedback about your experience, you can help us improve our services.

We will send your feedback to the appropriate area within the CRA, where it will be reviewed.

Service feedback may be about:

  • Employee behaviour
  • Service delays or expectations/ Promptness of services
  • Mistakes that could result in a misunderstanding or omission
  • Quality of information you receive or access
  • Accessibility barriers
  • other service-related issues
If your feedback is not about service

If you have feedback for the CRA that is not about a service experience, you have the following options:

  • If you disagree with or do not understand a letter, notice, payment, or other document you received from the CRA, ask the area it originated from for an explanation.
  • If you want to dispute an assessment, determination or decisions, see file an objection
  • If you disagree with the income Tax Act or other tax legislation matter, contact the Department of Finance Canada

If your feedback is not for the CRA, refer to Canada.ca.

Feedback confirmation

If your feedback is a complaint or you request a reply, you should receive a phone call or letter of acknowledgement within 15 business days of the CRA receiving your feedback.

After you submit feedback

What happens after you submit

Your service feedback will be sent to the appropriate area within the CRA, where it will be reviewed. A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process.

Processing time

How long it takes to resolve your complaint can vary depending on the complexity of your situation.

Our goal is to resolve complaints within 30 business days of the date we receive them. We will mail you a letter to explain how the issue was resolved.

Check the CRA processing times

If you are not satisfied with our response

You may contact the Office of the Taxpayer’s Ombudsperson if you are not satisfied with the response you received.

The Ombudsperson will only respond to complaints that CRA has already tried to resolve.

Contact the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson

Know your tax rights

You can expect to be treated fairly and get a high level of service each time you deal with the CRA. All information you give to the CRA will be treated as confidential under the Privacy Act.

For details: Taxpayer Bill of Rights
