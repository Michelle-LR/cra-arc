Types of feedback

There are 3 types of feedback you can give the CRA:

  • Compliment - A positive experience you had with the CRA
  • Suggestion - Ideas on how to improve the CRA
  • Complaint - A problem you had with the CRA

Service feedback

Steps to submit service feedback

  1. Call the CRA

    Resolve your concerns by calling:

    • CRA employee you have been dealing with
    • Telephone number provided in your CRA correspondence

    If no number is provided, contact the CRA.

  2. File a service feedback form

    If calling did not address your concern, use 1 of these methods to submit a service feedback form:

    Online digital form : Option 1 of 2

    Complete and submit your feedback with the Online digital service feedback form - RC193

    PDF form : Option 2 of 2

    Fill out your form on the PDF version - RC193

    Ways to submit your PDF form to the CRA

    Online with 1 of the CRA sign in services

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client

    By fax

    • 1-866-388-7371 from Canada or the United States.
    • 1-819-536-0701 from outside Canada and the United States.

    By mail

    CRA Service Feedback
    National Intake Centre
    4695 Shawinigan-Sud Boulevard
    Shawinigan QC  G9P 5H9
    CANADA

  3. Send a letter

    You can send a letter to the CRA.

After you submit your service feedback

Once we receive your feedback it will be forwarded to the:

  • Responsible manager and/or employee for recognition
  • Appropriate office or program area for consideration / review

A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process.

Feedback processing times

The CRA service standard to resolve your feedback is within 30 business days after receiving it. Time to resolve a complaint will depend on the complexity of the request.

For details: Check the CRA processing times

Contact the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson

If you are still not satisfied with how the CRA handled your service complaint, you can contact the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson. Generally, the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson will not review a service complaint until the CRA has finished its review.

Non-service feedback

These are other types of complaints or problems not related to service.

Some examples:

  • Disagree with or not understanding a letter, notice, payment, or other document received from the CRA
  • Dispute an assessment, determination, or decision made by the CRA
  • Disagree with the Income Tax Act, or tax legislation matters

Your rights as a taxpayer

You can expect to be treated fairly and get a high level of service each time you deal with the CRA. All information you give to the CRA will be treated as confidential under the Privacy Act.

For details: Taxpayer Bill of Rights
