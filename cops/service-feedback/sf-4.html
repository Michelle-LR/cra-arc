What is service feedback

Service feedback can be a complaint, suggestion or compliment about a service experience you had with the CRA. By submitting feedback about your experience, you can helps us improve our services.

Includes feedback on CRA services like:

  • mistakes resulting in a misunderstanding or omission
  • undue delays (see service standards)
  • employee behaviour
  • poor or misleading information
  • accessibility barriers
  • other service-related issues

Not included:

Actions you can take for non-service related complaints

These are other types of complaints or problems not related to service and the actions you can take.

Before you submit a complaint about delays, see our service standards.

If you disagree with or do not understand a letter, notice, payment, or other document you received from the CRA, ask the area it originated from for an explanation. For more information, go to Contact the Canada Revenue Agency.

To dispute an assessment, determination, or decision, see File an objection.

If you disagree with the Income Tax Act or other tax legislation matters, contact the Department of Finance Canada. The CRA administers tax legislation. The Department of Finance Canada is responsible for developing tax laws.

Submit service feedback

There are several options to submit your service feedback.

Talk to us

You have the option to call the CRA first to try and resolve any concerns by phone.

You may call the CRA employee you have been dealing with (or their supervisor) to discuss your issue, or call the phone number provided in the CRA correspondence.

Digital form

Complete and submit your feedback fastest and easiest way, with the online digital service feedback form - RC193. This option does not allow you to attach additional documents.

Fillable PDF or by letter

Complete the fillable PDF RC193 form or write a letter.

Ways to send your PDF or letter to the CRA

Confirmation

If your feedback is a complaint or you request a reply, you should receive a phone call or letter of acknowledgement within 15 days of the CRA receiving your feedback.

After you submit feedback

If you request a response to your service feedback, you should receive a phone call or letter of acknowledgement within 15 days of the CRA receiving it. Your feedback will be forwarded to the:

  • Responsible manager and/or employee for recognition
  • Appropriate office or program area for consideration / review

A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process.

Processing times

Our goal is to resolve your feedback within 30 business days from the date it is received.

Time to resolve a complaint can vary depending on the complexity of the request.

For details: Check the CRA processing times

Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson

If you are still not satisfied with how the CRA handled your service complaint, you can contact the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson. Generally, the Office of the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson will not review a service complaint until the CRA has finished its review.

Know your tax rights

You can expect to be treated fairly and get a high level of service each time you deal with the CRA. All information you give to the CRA will be treated as confidential under the Privacy Act.

For details: Taxpayer Bill of Rights
