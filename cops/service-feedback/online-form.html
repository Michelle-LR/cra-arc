Use the Service Feedback – RC193 online form to provide feedback on your service experience with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

Your feedback may be about:

  • A service complaint, suggestion, or compliment
  • An accessibility barrier or the CRA Accessibility Plan

You cannot submit additional supporting documents using this form.

If we need any additional information from you, we will ask for it when we contact you.

After you submit this form, print or save a copy of the confirmation page for your records.

What is service feedback

Service feedback can be a service complaint, suggestion or compliment about an experience you had with the CRA. We will send the feedback you submit to the appropriate area within the CRA, where it will be reviewed.

By submitting feedback about your experience, you can help us improve our services.

Service feedback is about your interaction with the CRA

Feedback may be about any of the following:

  • Employee behaviour
  • Delays beyond current processing times
  • Poor or misleading information
  • Mistakes that could result in a misunderstanding
  • Suggestions to improve service
  • Compliments about good service
  • Other service-related feedback

For feedback about accessibility: Submit accessibility feedback

If your feedback is not about service

If you have feedback for the CRA that is not about a service experience, you have the following options:

  • If you disagree with or do not understand a letter, notice, payment, or other document you received from the CRA, ask the area it originated from for an explanation
  • If you want to dispute an assessment, determination or decisions, see File an objection
  • If you disagree with the income Tax Act or other tax legislation matters, contact the Department of Finance Canada

If your feedback is not about the CRA, contact the government department in question at Canada.ca.

Before you submit feedback

It may be helpful to check CRA processing times or call us before submitting feedback. You may call the employee you have been dealing with (or their supervisor) to discuss your concerns, or call the phone number provided in the CRA correspondence you received.

If you don’t have a contact number, you may look up the number of the responsible program area on the CRA Contact page.
