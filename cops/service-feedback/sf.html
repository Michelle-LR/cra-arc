Types of feedback

There are 3 types of feedback you can give the CRA.

Compliment

Submit a positive experience you had with the CRA

Suggestion

Give your idea on how to improve the CRA

Complaint

Tell us about a problem you had with the CRA

Feedback about service you received

You have several ways to provide feedback on service you received from the CRA:

  • By phone - Call the CRA
  • By form - Complete the form
  • By contacting the Taxpayers' Ombudsperson

Methods to use

Online form : Option 1 of 2

Complete and submit your feedback with the Online service feedback application form - RC193

PDF form : Option 2 of 2

Fill out your form on the PDF version of the form - RC193

After you submit your feedback

Once we receive your feedback it will be forwarded to the:

  • Responsible manager and/or employee for recognition
  • Appropriate office or program area for consideration / review

A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process. Time to resolve a complaint will depend on the complexity of the request.

Feedback processing times

The CRA service standard to resolve the complaint is within 30 business days after receiving your complaint.

For details: Check the CRA processing times

Non-service feedback

These are other types of complaints or problems not related to service.

Some examples:

  • Disagree with or not understanding a letter, notice, payment, or other document received from the CRA
  • Dispute an assessment, determination, or decision made by the CRA
  • Disagree with the Income Tax Act, or tax legislation matters

Your rights

You can expect to be treated fairly and get a high level of service each time you deal with the CRA. All information you give to the CRA will be treated as confidential under the Privacy Act.

For details: Taxpayer Bill of Rights
