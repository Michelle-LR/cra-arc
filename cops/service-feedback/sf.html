Types of feedback

There are 3 type of feedback you can give the CRA.

Compliment

You can submit feedback about a positive experience with the service that you received from the CRA. Your feedback will be forwarded onto the responsible manager and/or employee for recognition. A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process.

Suggestion

You can submit a feedback on a suggestion about improving the CRA service(s). Your feedback will be forwarded to the appropriate office or program area for consideration. A service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail as part of the review process.

Complaint

You can submit feedback about a complaint about the service that you received from the CRA. When reviewing your complaint, a service feedback officer may contact you by phone or by mail. How long it takes to resolve your complaint depends on the complexity of the situation.

Your rights

You can expect to be treated fairly and get a high level of service each time you deal with the CRA. All information you give to the CRA will be treated as confidential under the Privacy Act.
