Determine the filing due date

Based on information return type

T4, T4A, T4A(OAS), T4A(P), T4A-NR, T4E, T4RSP, T4RIF, T5, T5007, T5008, AGR-1, TFSA, T4FHSA, T2202

The filing due date is the last day of February after the preceding calendar year

T3, T1061, T2000, T3D, T3GR, T3P, T3PRP, T3RI 90 days after the end of the trust's tax year

T1204 March 31 after the preceding calendar year

T5013

The due date for filing an annual return (including distributing the T5013 slips) depends on the type of partners, including end members of a tiered partnership.

  • March 31 after the calendar year in which the fiscal period of the partnership ended if, throughout the fiscal period:
    • all partners are individuals, including end members of tiered partnership, and investment clubs that file on the modified-partnership basis. We consider a trust to be an individual
  • 5 months after the end of the partnership's fiscal period if, throughout the fiscal period:
    • all partners are corporations, including end members of a tiered partnership
  • In all other cases, the earlier of:
    • March 31 after the calendar year in which the fiscal period of the partnership ended
    • the day that is 5 months after the end of the partnership's fiscal period
T5018 6 months after the end of the reporting period (calendar year or fiscal period) chosen by the payer

NR4 March 31 after the preceding calendar year or 90 days after the end of the trust or estate tax year

RRSP Contribution Information Return, Pooled Registered Pension Plan Information Return

File by May 1 of the year in which the contribution year ends. The contribution year is the period beginning on the 61st day of one year and ending on the 60th day of the following year.
Part XVIII, Part XIX

File by May 1 following the calendar year to which the information return applies.

When is an information return considered on time

The CRA considers the information return to be filed on time if it is received on or before the due date. When the due date falls on a Saturday, a Sunday, or a public holiday recognized by the CRA, your information return is considered on time if the CRA receives it or it is postmarked on or before the next business day.

What happens if you file late or you do not file

Legislated penalties

You may be charged a penalty if you file your information return late. To learn more, see late-filing penalties for information returns. Each slip is an information return, and the penalty the CRA assesses is based on the number of information returns you filed late. The penalty is $100 or the amount calculated according to the chart below, whichever is more:

Relieving administrative policy

For specific information returns, the administrative policy reduces the penalty that you may be charged to make sure it is fair and reasonable for small businesses. You may be charged a reduced penalty if you file your information return late and it is part of the list of specific information returns that applies to the administrative policy. Each slip is an information return, and the penalty the CRA assesses is based on the number of information returns you filed late. Additional legislative penalty for conspiring in order to avoid reporting contract payments (T5018 information returns)

Any contractor that plans with subcontractors to avoid tax by concealing underground activity could face criminal prosecution, with fines and penalties of up to 200% of the tax they tried to avoid.
