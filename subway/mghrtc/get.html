Determining the amount to claim

An eligible individual can claim up to $50,000 in qualifying expenditures for a qualifying renovation that was completed in the tax year.

Only one qualifying renovation can be claimed during the lifetime of a qualifying individual.

The MHRTC is 15% of whichever is less:

  • $50,000

  • The amount of qualifying expenditures incurred by the eligible individual

    Refer to: Expenses you can claim

Since this tax credit is refundable, you will receive a refund if the credit you claim is more than the taxes you owe.

Example: Claiming the maximum amount of the MHRTC

Jackie is eligible to claim the MHRTC on her 2023 return. Her qualifying expenditures are $70,000.

Even though Jackie paid $70,000 for expenses, only the maximum of $50,000 can be claimed:

  • $50,000 in qualifying expenses
  • xtimes 15% (lowest personal income tax rate)
  • =equals $7,500 refundable tax credit

When Jackie calculated her taxes before including the MHRTC, she had a balance owing of $2,000. With the maximum MHRTC applied, she will receive a refund of $5,500.

If you have more than one qualifying renovation in a year

If you complete two qualifying renovations for two separate qualifying individuals, you can calculate and claim the MHRTC for each one.

Example: Completing two qualifying renovations in the same year

Des is related to three qualifying individuals: her brother, her mother, and her father.

In April 2023, she renovated her house to create a self-contained basement apartment so her brother could move in with her. This renovation was completed in October 2023. Her qualifying expenses for this renovation were $15,000.

In July 2023, Des decided to build a second separate apartment over the garage for her elderly parents to move into. This renovation was completed in December 2023. Her qualifying expenses for this renovation were $63,000.

As long as both renovations meet the eligibility criteria, Des may claim the expenses for both qualifying renovations on her 2023 tax return. The claims must be calculated separately to determine the maximum amount for each:

Renovation 1

  • $15,000 in qualifying expenses
  • xtimes 15% (lowest personal income tax rate)
  • =equals $2,250 refundable tax credit

Renovation 2

  • $50,000 in qualifying expenses (maximum)
  • xtimes 15% (lowest personal income tax rate)
  • =equals $7,500 refundable tax credit

Total MHRTC for 2023: $9,750

Splitting the credit between eligible individuals

When more than one individual incurs expenses for the same qualifying renovation, the credit may be shared as long as all of the following conditions are met:

  • Each person claiming the MHRTC is an eligible individual

    Refer to: Who can claim

  • The total amount claimed between all eligible individuals is not more than the maximum
  • The eligible individuals claim only qualifying expenditures that they incurred
  • No MHRTC has previously been claimed for the qualifying individual (the person 65 years of age or older, or adult who qualifies for the disability tax credit)
Example: Splitting the maximum credit between two eligible individuals

Rey and Jax shared the cost of a qualifying renovation in 2023. Both Rey and Jax are eligible individuals and may claim their portion of the qualifying expenses on their returns.

The total amount of qualifying expenses Rey and Jax incurred for the renovation was $62,000.

Rey’s qualifying expenses were $29,000.

Jax’s qualifying expenses were $33,000.

Because the maximum amount of expenses that can be claimed for the MHRTC is $50,000, Rey and Jax will split the credit according to the portion of expenses each of them incurred.

Rey's claim:

  • Rey’s portion of maximum claim: $29,000 ÷ $62, 000 = 47%
  • Amount of expenses Rey will claim: $50,000 × 47% = $23,500

    Jax's claim:

  • Jax’s portion of maximum claim: $33,000 ÷ $62, 000 = 53%
  • Amount of expenses Jax will claim: $50,000 × 53% = $26,500
