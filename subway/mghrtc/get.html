Determining the amount to claim

An eligible individual can claim up to $50,000 in qualifying expenditures for a qualifying renovation that was completed in the tax year.

Only one qualifying renovation is permitted during the lifetime of a qualifying individual.

The MHRTC is 15% of whichever amount is less:

  • $50,000

  • The amount of qualifying expenditures incurred by the eligible individual

    Refer to: Expenses you can claim

  • $0, if the individual is not a resident of Canada at any time during the tax year

    Residency status if there is a death

    The deceased individual is deemed to be:

    • a resident of Canada from the time of death until the end of the year if, immediately before death, the deceased individual was resident in Canada

When you complete your tax return, you will use schedule 12 to calculate the amount of MHRTC you can claim.

Refer to: How to claim

Since this is a refundable tax credit, if the credit you can claim is higher than the taxes you owe, you'll receive the difference as a refund.

Example: Claiming the maximum amount of the MHRTC

Jackie is eligible to claim the MHRTC on her 2023 tax return. Her qualifying expenditures totaled $70,000.

Even though her qualifying expenditures totaled $70,000, only the maximum of $50,000 can be claimed:

When Jackie calculated her taxes before including the MHRTC, she had a balance owing of $2000. With the maximum MHRTC applied, she will now receive a refund of $5500.

Splitting the credit between eligible individuals

More than one individual may be eligible to claim expenses they incurred for a qualifying renovation. In this case, the credit may be shared, as long as all of the following are true:

  • Each person claiming part of the MHRTC is eligible to claim the credit

    Refer to: Who can claim

  • The total amount claimed between all eligible individuals does not exceed the maximum
  • The eligible individuals agree on how much each of them will claim
  • No MHRTC has previously been claimed for that qualifying individual (the person aged 65 or older or adult who qualifies for the Disability Tax Credit)
