How to claim

You can claim the MHRTC when you complete your income tax and benefit return for the renovation period tax year.

When to claim

You only make the claim once. The claim is made in the taxation year that the renovation period ends. The "renovation period" is a period that:

  • Begins at the time that the first qualifying expenditure is made or incurred for a qualifying renovation (for example, when you apply for a building permit)
  • Ends at the time of the completion of the qualifying renovation (for example, when the qualifying renovation passes the final inspection or proof of project completion is received)

The renovation period tax year is the tax year that includes the end of the renovation period of the qualifying renovation.

Example: Renovation ends in 2024

Regardless of when the renovations started, it can only be claimed once, in the year it ended. In a situation where the renovation is completed in 2024, it means the renovation expenses are claim on the 2024 tax return.

Claim on the tax return

For 2023 and subsequent taxation years the MHRTC can be claimed on the T1 income tax and benefit return, line 45355.

Example: If an expense has already been claimed

Expenses are not eligible for the to be claimed more than once. For example, if the expense was already claimed under the medical expense tax credit (METC) or the home accessibility tax credit (HATC), you cannot claim it under the Multigenerational home renovation tax credit (MHRTC).

The person who makes the claim is person who incurred the renovation expenses.

If more than one eligible individual makes an MHRTC claim

If more than one eligible individual makes an MHRTC claim for the same qualifying renovation, the total of all amounts claimed must not be more than $50,000. If the individuals cannot agree on what portion of the amounts each can claim, the Minister of National Revenue may decide the portions.

Claiming electronically verus on paper

Electronic filing
To claim on an electronic return, follow the instructions in your certified tax software for 2023.
Paper filing
To claim on paper return, complete and attach Schedule 12, Multigenerational Home Renovation Tax Credit, included in your 2023 tax package.

Keep your supporting documation

The documents must clearly identify the type and number of goods bought or services provided, such as the following:

  • Information that clearly identifies the vendor/contractor, their business address, and, if applicable, their GST/HST registration number
  • A description of the goods and the date they were bought
  • The date when the goods were delivered (keep your delivery slip as proof) or when the work or services were performed, or both
  • A description of the work done, including the address
  • The amount of the invoice
  • Proof of payment
    (Receipts or invoices must indicate that they are paid in full or be accompanied by other proof of payment, such as a credit card slip or cancelled cheque)

To verify whether someone is registered for GST/HST, refer to: GST/HST Registry

Impacts on other benefits

Expenses that may be included in an MHRTC claim must be reduced by any expenses that can reasonably be considered to have been reimbursed. This includes any form of assistance that you receive, or are entitled to receive, including any related rebates, such as those for GST/HST.
