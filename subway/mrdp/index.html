Reporting Rules for Digital Platform Operators

The new Reporting Rules for Digital Platform Operators affect the digital economy and its participants, particularly those in the sharing and gig economy.

The rules apply to certain platform operators which enable third-party users (sellers) to generate income through the sale of goods or provision of certain services. These platform operators have to report detailed information to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) annually on certain sellers. 

Sellers who are reported to CRA may include those who participate following economies:

  • Sale of goods
  • Real property rental services (both residential and commercial)
  • Ride-share, vehicle rental and delivery services
  • Personal services
    What are personal services

    Time or task based work performed by one or more individuals at the request of a user. Work that is purely ancillary to the overall transaction or that is provided by a seller pursuant to an employment relationship with the platform operator or related entity is not a personal service.

Source of the new rules

The rules received royal assent on June 22, 2023 and are contained in Part XX of the Income Tax Act and are based on the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development’s Model Rules for Reporting by Platform Operators with respect to Sellers in the Sharing and Gig Economy.
