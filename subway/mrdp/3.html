What information is shared

Customers

If you are only buying products or services using digital platforms (not selling or facilitating sales), the new requirements do not apply to you. You do not have to share, report, or file anything. Your browsing and buying information is also not shared with the CRA.

Sellers of products or services

If you are a seller of products or services who is considered to be a reportable seller, your information will be collected by the platform and reported to the CRA. This information is referred to as reportable elements.

Reportable elements

  • Identification information

    • name of seller (first and last name of an individual; legal name of an entity)
    • seller’s primary address
    • seller’s jurisdiction(s) of residence for tax purposes
    • seller’s TIN and jurisdiction in which the TIN was issued
      What is a tax identification number

      Tax identification number – In Canada, a tax identification number includes a social insurance number, a business number or an account number issued to a trust.

    • any other TIN for the seller that is available to the platform operator, including jurisdiction of issuance
    • date of birth (for an individual)
    • business registration number (for an entity)
    • any financial account identifiers (such as bank account numbers) for the seller that are available to the platform operator
    • the name of the holder of the financial account to which the consideration is paid or credited (if different from the name of the seller and if available to the platform operator), along with any other identifying information in respect of that account holder available to the platform operator

  • Activity information

    • total consideration paid or credited to the seller in the reporting period in respect of relevant activities (broken down by calendar quarter) and the number of such relevant activities
    • any fees, commissions or taxes withheld or charged by the platform operator (broken down by calendar quarter)
    • where the seller rented immovable property,
      • the address of each property listing and if available, the land registration number
      • where available, the number of days each property listing was rented during the reporting period and the type of each property listing

These digital platforms will provide you with a copy of the annual information that is collected and reported under these rules by January 31 of each year. 

The information reported to the CRA may be shared with tax authorities in other jurisdictions that have adopted similar rules. In addition, the CRA will receive information about Canadian sellers or rental property located in Canada from other jurisdictions that have implemented similar reporting rules as Canada for platforms operating in their jurisdiction.

For more information about the reportable elements, refer to: Chapter 6 of the CRA's Guidance on the Reporting Rules for Digital Platform Operators

Expect the platform to ask for your infomation

As a result of these rules, digital platforms on which you are selling may ask you for additional information about you or your business in order to fulfill their reporting obligations to the CRA.

Consequences for not providing required information

You may face consequences from both your platform operator(s) and the CRA if you do not provide the required information:

  • If you do not provide the information your platform operator(s) are required to report, they can take actions against you like locking your account.
  • If you do not provide your tax identification number to your platform operator(s), the CRA may assess you a penalty of $500.

Other tax obligations

For more information about your tax obligations as an online seller, refer to: the CRA’s Taxes and the platform economy

Digital platform operators

If you are a digital platform operator who is considered to be a reporting platform operator, you must collect and report the following information under the rules:

  • Information about you (and your businesses):
    • Legal name
    • Registered office address
    • Taxpayer identification number (TIN)
      What is a tax identification number

      Tax identification number – In Canada, a tax identification number includes a social insurance number, a business number or an account number issued to a trust.

    • Business names of all platforms for which you’re reporting
  • Identification and activity information (reportable elements) about reportable sellers

You must also provide each reportable seller with a copy of the annual information  reported to the CRA about them by January 31 of each year.

A reporting platform operator does not have to report the required information concerning a seller to the CRA if they have obtained adequate assurances that:

  • Another platform operator has or will fulfil that reporting obligation according to the rules in Canada

  • The seller is not resident in Canada, under substantially similar rules in a partner jurisdiction

For more details, refer to: CRA's Guidance on the Reporting Rules for Digital Platform Operators

Due diligence procedures

As a reporting platform operator, you must take steps to verify the accuracy of the information you collect and report under the rules.

More specifically, you must verify all of the following:

  • excluded sellers
  • the reliability of seller information
  • in the case of rental property, verify the address of each property listing 

These steps must be completed by December 31 of the reportable period to which they relate, except for transitional periods (i.e. the first year a platform operator is considered a reporting platform operator under the rules).

For more information about due diligence procedures, refer to: Chapter 5 of the CRA's Guidance on the Reporting Rules for Digital Platform Operators
