Who it applies to

Customers

If you are only buying products or services using digital platforms (not selling or facilitating sales), the new requirements do not apply to you. You do not have to share, report, or file anything. Your browsing and buying information is also not shared with the CRA.

Sellers of products or services

When the rules apply

If you are a seller of products or services, the rules apply to you if you are considered to be a reportable seller.

A reportable seller is all of the following:

  • An individual or entity that is an active seller
    What is an active seller

    The seller must have provided relevant services or sold goods during the reportable period (calendar year) or has been paid or credited compensation in connection with relevant activities during the reportable period.

  • Has been determined by a reporting platform operator to be one of the following:  
    • Be resident in a reportable jurisdiction
    • Have provided relevant services for the rental of real or immovable property located in a reportable jurisdiction
    • Have been paid or credited consideration in connection with relevant services for the rental of real or immovable property located in a reportable jurisdiction
    What is a platform operator

    Under the new requirements, a platform operator is someone who contracts with sellers to make all or part of a platform (for example (for example, software such as a website or application) available to them to connect with buyers. For the new rules to apply , the platform must enable the provision of specific supplies (referred to as relevant activities) and must include the collection and payment of consideration in respect of those supplies.

    Software that exclusively allows for the following activities is not considered a platform under the rules:

    • the processing of payments
    • listing or advertising
    • redirecting or transferring of users to another platform
    What is a reportable jurisdiction

    A reportable jurisdiction depends on the jurisdiction of residence of the reporting platform operator. If the platform operator is resident in Canada, then Canada and any partner jurisdiction is a reportable jurisdiction. This means the platform operator is required to report to Canada information about the following:

    • sellers resident in Canada
    • sellers resident in any partner jurisdiction
    • sellers renting immovable property located in Canada or any partner jurisdiction

    In any other case, only Canada is a reportable jurisdiction. This means that such a platform operator (for example, a platform operator resident outside Canada) would be required to report to Canada only information about sellers resident in Canada and sellers renting immovable property located in Canada.

  • Is not an excluded seller
When the rules don't apply

If you are a seller of products or services, the rules do not apply to you if you are considered to be an excluded seller.

An excluded seller is one of the following:

  • An entity for which the reporting platform operator facilitated more than 2,000 relevant services for the rental of immovable property in respect of a property listing during the reportable period
  • A government entity
  • An entity that has stock of which is regularly traded on an established securities market or a related entity of an entity that has stock of which is regularly traded on an established securities market or
  • An individual or entity for which the platform operator has solely facilitated less than 30 relevant activities for the sale of goods and for which the total amount of consideration paid or credited did not exceed $2,800 during the reportable period 

Digital platform operators

When the rules apply

If you are a digital platform operator who is considered to be a reporting platform operator, the rules apply to you.

A reporting platform operator is an operator that is not excluded from the reporting rules and:

  • is resident in Canada
  • is resident, incorporated or managed in a “partner jurisdiction”, facilitates the provision of relevant activities by sellers resident in Canada or with respect to rental of immovable property located in Canada and elects to be reporting platform operator or
  • is not resident in Canada or a partner jurisdiction, and it facilitates the provision of relevant activities by sellers resident in Canada or with respect to rental of immovable property located in Canada
What is relevant activity

Relevant activity means:

  • the sale of goods, or
  • the following services (referred to as relevant services)
    • the rental of real or immovable property
    • a personal service or
    • the rental of a means of transport
What is personal service

Time or task based work performed by one or more individuals at the request of a user. Work that is purely ancillary to the overall transaction or that is provided by a seller pursuant to an employment relationship with the platform operator or related entity is not a personal service.

When the rules don't apply

Platform operators do not have to report the required information if they can demonstrate that their entire business model does not:

  • allow sellers to derive a profit
  • have reportable sellers
