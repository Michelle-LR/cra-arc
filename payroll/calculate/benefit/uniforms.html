Uniforms and special clothing

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit to your employee for distinctive clothing, protective clothing, safety footwear or safety glasses, including the cost related to laundry or dry cleaning to your employee.

  • References

Steps

Generally, if you provide to your employee for distintive clothing, protective clothing, safety footwear or safety glasses, or an allowance , or you reimburse , including the cost related to laundry or dry cleaning, the benefit is taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

  1. Determine if the benefit provided is considered a distinctive uniform or protecting clothing

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, the benefit is considered a distinctive uniform or protecting clothing if one of the following:

    • Distinctive uniform

      • Your employee must wear while carrying out the employment duties
      • Your employee cannot wear the clothing for personal purposes, for example, outside of business hours

    • Protective clothing, safety footwear and safety glasses

      • Your employee is required by law to wear it on the work site
      • It is designed to protect your employee from hazards associated with their employment

    • If the benefit does not meet one of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

      Continue to: Step 3 - Calculate the value of the benefit

    • If the benefit is considered a distinctive uniform or protecting clothing, continue to: Step 2 - Determine if the benefit provided is taxable

  2. Determine if the benefit provided is taxable

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if the benefit provided is for a distinctive uniform, protective clothing, safety footwear or safety glasses, including the cost related to laundry or dry cleaning, the benefit is not taxable if one of the following apply:

    • You provide an allowance and all of the following apply:

      • The benefit provided meets all conditions in step 1
      • The amount of the allowance is reasonable and you can justify your position

    • You reimburse or provide an accountable advance and all of the following apply:

      • The benefit provided meets all conditions in step 1
      • The cost is reasonable and you can justify your position
      • You obtained receipts from your employee to document the purchases
    • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations. Do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to: Step 3 - Calculate the value of the benefit

  3. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

    • Fair market value of the benefit received or enjoyed
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  4. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  5. Report the benefit on a slip
