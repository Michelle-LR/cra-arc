Moving and relocation expenses

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may reimburse or pay on behalf of your employees for the cost of moving or relocation.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the move of your employee is an eligible relocation

    The move of your employee is an eligible relocation if all of the following apply:

    • Your employee changed their place of residence in Canada to take a job in a new location in Canada. For example, your employee:
      • was transferred from one of your places of business to another
      • accepted employment at a different location from the location of their former residence
      • finished an employment contract in a remote area
    • The move enables your employee to be at least 40 kilometres by the shortest public route closer to your new work location
    • Your employee's old residence and new residence are both in Canada
    • Kilometres between new residence and new work location
    • minus Kilometres between old residence and new work location
    • equals Kilometres must be at least 40 kilometres
    • If the move is not an eligible location, the benefit is taxable. Continue to: Step 3 - Calculate the value of the benefit
    • If the move is an eligible relocation, continue to: Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable

  2. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    Generally, moving or relocation allowances or reimbursements you provide to your employees are taxable.

    Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Situations

    Situation: You provide an accountable allowance, a non-accountable allowance, a reimbursement or payment to your employment for moving expenses

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you provide an accountable allowance, a non-accountable allowance, a reimbursement or payment to your employment for moving expenses, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • In respect of relocation to one of your place of business or relocation away from a remote work location after the employment is finished
    • Allowance is $650 or less
    • Employee certifies in writing that they incurred moving expenses for at least the amount of the allowance (your employee does not have to provide details or submit receipts to justify the amounts paid)

    Taxable situation

    If the allowance or reimbursement you provide to your employee for moving or relocation expenses do not meet all of the conditions above, the full amount is a taxable benefit.

    Situation: You provide a reimbursement to your employee in respect to housing lost for moving or relocation expenses

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you provide a reimbursement to your employee for moving or relocation expenses, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • In respect of relocation to one of your place of business or relocation away from a remote work location after the employment is finished
    • In respect of an eligible housing loss, this means you and the employee do not deal at arm's length
    • Allowance is less than $15,000

    Taxable situation

    If the reimbursement you provide to your employee for moving or relocation expenses:

    • Do not meet all of the conditions above, the full amount is a taxable benefit
    • Do not meet all of the conditions above because the eligible housing loss is more than $15,000, the benefit is partly taxable (benefit is equal to half of the difference between the amount paid in respect of the eligible housing loss and $15,000)

  3. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the payments for the moving or relocation expense
    • minus $15,000 exemption limit for eligible housing loss (step 1b, if applicable)
    • equals Result
    • multiply by 50% of the total of the amounts paid that exceeds the exemption limit for housing loss (step 1b, if applicable)
    • equals Value of the taxable benefit for the moving or relocation expense
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
    Example 1 - Calculations

    Employer paid the employee $20,000 in compensation for the loss they incurred when the employee sold their home to move for their work in Ontario. The loss is not an eligible housing loss because the employee's new residence was only 25 kilometres closer to their new work location than the employee's old residence.

    • $20,000 is the total value of the moving expenses that is taxable
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $20,000 is the value of the benefit to be included on the employee's T4 slip

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 2 - Calculations

    An employee started to work at the employer's new work location on December 1, 2021. In June 2022, the adjusted cost base of the employee's residence was $427,801 in Ontario. The FMV of the residence remained stable at $425,000 during the six months preceding June 2022. In May 2022, the employee sold their residence for a cost of $362,801. In June 2022, the employer paid the employee $65,000 in compensation for the eligible housing loss incurred following the sale of the employee's house. In June 2017, Paul's employer paid him $65,000 in compensation for the EHL incurred following the sale of his house. The employer paid out the compensation in two payments: $30,000 in June 2021 and $35,000 in January 2022.

    • 2021 calculations
      • $30,000 is the June 2021 total payment for the moving or relocation expense
      • minus $15,000 is the exemption limit for the housing loss
      • equals $15,000 is the result
      • multiply by 50%
      • equals $7,500 is the value of the taxable benefit for the eligible housing loss
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $7,500 is the value of the benefit to be included on the employee's T4 slip in June 2021
    • 2022 calculation
      • $65,000 is the total payments for the moving or relocation expense
      • minus $15,000 is the exemption limit for the housing loss
      • equals $25,000 is the result
      • minus $7,500 is the value of the benefit that was included on the employee's T4 slip in June 2021
      • equals $17,500 is the value of the taxable benefit
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $17,500 is the value of the benefit to be included on the employee's T4 slip in January 2022

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  4. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  5. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Housing
      • Code 40 - Other Information

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Housing
      • Code 40 - Other Information
