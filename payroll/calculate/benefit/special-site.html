Board, lodging and transportation expenses - Provided to an employee in special work sites

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit for board, lodging and transportation expenses to your employee who works or lives in a location that is in a special work site.

Generally, a special work site is an area where temporary duties are performed by an employee who keeps a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE) at another location as their principal place of residence.

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit is considered in a special situation

    The benefit may not be taxable if you provide to your employee is in one of the following special situations:

    • Refer to: Your employee is working in a prescribed zone
    • Refer to: Your employee has a severe or prolonged impairment

    It is possible for an employee to work at a location that is both a special work site and a remote work location. However, the benefit can only be excluded from the employee's income once.

    • If the benefit is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is not in one of the above special situations, continue to Step 2 - Determine if the duties performed are considered temporary.

  2. Determine if the duties performed are considered temporary

    The duties performed are considered temporary if they are performed on a short-term or interim basis.

    Generally, the CRA considers that an employee's duties is temporary if they are unlikely to provide continuous employment for the employee for more than two years.

    • If the duties performed are not considered temporary, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the duties performed are considered temporary, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if the principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE)

  3. Determine if the principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE)

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 2, your employee's principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE) if the following apply:

    • A dwelling-house, apartment, or other similar place of residence in which place a person as a general rule sleeps and eats
    • Generally a living unit with restricted access that contains a kitchen, bathroom, and sleeping facilities
    • Separate from any other living unit in the same building.

    Generally, a room in a hotel, dormitory, boarding house, or bunkhouse is not considered a SCDE.

    • If the principal place of residence is not considered a SCDE, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the principal place of residence is considered a SCDE, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if the distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable

  4. Determine if the distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 3, the CRA generally considers that it is not reasonable for an employee to travel between their principal place of residence and the workplace if the distance between those two places by the most direct route normally taken is at least 80 kilometres.

    Even if the distance is less than 80 kilometres, the CRA may still consider that it is not reasonable for the employee to make that trip after taking into account the following factors:

    • Condition of the road taken
    • Available means of transportation
    • Number of hours of work required of the employee
    • Time the employee would have for rest if he or she returned home every day
    • Employee's general state of physical and mental health
    • Time it takes to travel the distance, and the time of day the travel takes place
    • If the distance is considered reasonable, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the distance is considered unreasonable, continue to: Step 5 - Determine if your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours

  5. Determine if your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 4, your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours if all of the following apply:

    • Your employee's duties require them to be away from their principal place of residence or at a special work site for a period of more than 36 hours
    • Your employee had to work at the special work site for a period of more than 36 hours
    Special work sites specific to fishers and seafarers

    A ship may qualify as a special work site when it is at sea for a period of 36 hours or more. Time at sea may include time spent on board because of:

    • Quarantine
    • Waiting for docking
    • Unloading cargo
    • Immigration or customs clearance

    Your employee may qualify for exemption from tax on the value of, or a reasonable allowance for, board and lodging received while on a ship. If all conditions are met, a ship will qualify as a special work site at any time the employee is on board, whether it is in port or at sea.

    • If your employee was do not required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours, continue to: Step 6 - Fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site

  6. Fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site

    You and your employee must fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site, to exclude board, lodging and transportation from your employee's income. You must keep a copy.

    The TD4 requires the exact location of the work site and the distance to your employee's principal place of residence.

    • If you and your employee did not fill out Form TD4, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If you and your employee filled out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site, continue to: Step 7 - Determine if the benefit is taxable

  7. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    Generally, board, lodging and transportation allowances or reimbursements you provide to your employees are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you paid an allowance or reimburse your employee, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • The duties performed are considered temporary (step 2)
    • The principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE) (step 3)
    • The distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable (step 4)
    • Your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours (step 5)
    • Form TD4 is completed (step 6)
    • Allowance is reasonable
    • If the benefit is for transportation expenses, it was for a period during which the employee also received board and lodging benefits
    • If the benefit is for transportation expenses, it must relate to travel between the work location and the employee's principal place of residence
    • If the benefit is for transportation expenses, it must relate to travel between the work location and a location in Canada or in the country in which the employee is working

    Taxable situation

    If the board, lodging or transportation benefit does not meet all of the above conditions, the benefit is taxable.

  8. Calculate the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip

    The value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value for the board, lodging and transportation expenses
    • minus Value for the board and lodging expenses to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value for the transportation expenses to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  9. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  10. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following amounts on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, Transportation

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, Transportation
