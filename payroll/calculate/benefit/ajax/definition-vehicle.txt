<p>The difference between an <strong>automobile </strong>or a <strong>motor vehicle </strong> depends on:</p>

<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Seating capacity </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Type of vehicle </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Primary use of the vehicle </li>
</ul>
<p>As per the Income Tax Act:</p>
<ul>
<li>An <strong>automobile</strong> is defined as a motor vehicle that is designed or adapted mainly to carry individuals and their baggage on highways and streets, and has a seating capacity of not more than the driver and eight passengers. Vans and family cars belong to this category. Vehicles used for emergency response and vehicles used mainly to earn income are excluded from the definition of the term automobile: for example, ambulances, taxis and hearses.</li>
<li>A <strong>motor vehicle</strong> is defined as an automotive vehicle designed or adapted to be used on highways and streets but does not include trolley buses (tramway or streetcar) and any vehicle that operates exclusively on rails. </li>
</ul>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-xs-12">
<dl class="dl-horizontal">

<dt>More than 8 passengers, plus a driver</dt><dd> <p>The vehicle is considered a <strong>motor vehicle</strong>. </p>
</dd>
<dt>Less than 8 passengers, plus a driver</dt><dd> <p>The vehicle is considered a <strong>motor vehicle</strong> if <strong> one</strong> of the following apply:</p>
<ul>
<li>Primarily (50% or more) of the distance driven as a taxi </li>
<li>Primarily (50% or more) of the distance driven as a business of transporting passengers</li>
<li>Bought to sell, rent, or lease in a sales, rental, or leasing business</li>
<li>An ambulance</li>
<li>A clearly marked police or fire emergency response vehicle </li>
<li>A clearly marked emergency medical response vehicle that you use to carry emergency medical equipment and one or more emergency medical attendants or paramedics</li>
<li>A van, pick-up truck or similar type vehicle (function and design similar to pick-up truck or van) that can be used for the transportation of goods, equipment or passengers to earn or produce income, and one of the following apply:
<ul>
<li>Back part of the vehicle adapted so that the vehicle is unable to carry passengers</li>
<li>Used 50% to 89% or more at a remote work location (at least 30 kilometres away from any community having a population of at least 40,000)</li>
<li>Used 90% or more (in the year it was bought or leased) to transport goods or equipment in the course of business </li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>A hearse or other vehicle used in a funeral business to transport passengers</li>
</ul>

<p>A clearly marked emergency response vehicle means that the vehicle has decals and visible signs of being an emergency vehicle, including clearly marked police or fire vehicles and medical response vehicles, used to carry emergency medical equipment and one or more emergency medical attendants or paramedics.</p>	

<p>If the vehicle is <strong>not one</strong> of the above, it is considered an <strong>automobile</strong>. </p>   
</dd>
