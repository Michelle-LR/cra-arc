<p>The difference between an <strong>automobile </strong>or a <strong>motor vehicle </strong> depends on:</p>

<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and cnjnctn-sm">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Seating capacity </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Type of vehicle </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Primary use of the vehicle </li>
</ul>
<p>As per the Income Tax Act:</p>
<ul>
<li>An <strong>automobile</strong> is defined as a motor vehicle that is designed or adapted mainly to carry individuals on highways and streets, and has a seating capacity of not more than the driver and eight passengers.</li>
<li>A <strong>motor vehicle</strong> is defined as an automotive vehicle designed or adapted to be used on highways and streets but does not include trolley buses or a vehicle designed or adapted for use exclusively on rails. The CRA considers motor vehicle differently from automobile for income tax purposes.</li>
</ul>

<div class="row">
<div class="col-xs-12">
<dl class="dl-horizontal">

<dt>More than 8 passengers, plus a driver</dt><dd> <p>The vehicle is considered a <strong>motor vehicle</strong>. </p>
</dd>
<dt>8 passengers or less, plus a driver</dt><dd> <p>The vehicle is <strong>not</strong> considered an <strong>automobile</strong> if <strong> one</strong> of the following apply:</p>
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A vehicle you bought to use primarily (50% or more of the distance driven)  as a taxi</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A vehicle you bought to use primarily (50% or more of the distance driven) as a bus in the business of transporting passengers</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A vehicle you bought to use primarily (50% or more of the distance driven) as a hearse in a funeral business</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A vehicle bought to sell, rent, or lease in a sales, rental, or leasing business, except for benefits arising from personal use of an automobile</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A hearse used in the course of a business of arranging or managing funerals</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">An ambulance</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A clearly marked emergency response vehicle means that the vehicle has decals and visible signs of being an emergency vehicle, including clearly marked police or fire vehicles and medical response vehicles, used to carry emergency medical equipment and one or more emergency medical attendants or paramedics</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A van, pick-up truck or similar vehicle that is bought or leased in the year to be used in the course of business and <strong>one</strong> of the following apply:
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-or">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Can seat no more than the driver and two passengers, and is used (50% or more of the distance driven) to transport goods or equipment only</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Is used (90% or more of the distance driven) to transport goods, equipment, or passengers</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">A pickup truck that is bought or leased in the year and <strong>all</strong> of the following apply:
<ul class="list-unstyled cnjnctn-type-and">
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Used (50% or more of the distance driven) to transport goods, equipment, or passengers in the course of earning or producing income </li>
<li class="cnjnctn-col">Used at a remote work location or at a special work site that is at least 30 kilometres away from any community having a population of at least 40,000. 	<p>If the back part or trunk of a van, pickup truck, or similar vehicle has been permanently altered and can no longer be used as a passenger vehicle, it is no longer considered an automobile as long as it is used primarily for business.</p></li>
</ul>
</li>


</li>
</ul>

<p>If the vehicle is <strong>one</strong> of the above, it <strong>not</strong> is considered an <strong>automobile</strong>. </p>   
</dd>
