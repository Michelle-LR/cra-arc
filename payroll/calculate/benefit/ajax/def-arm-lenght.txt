<dt>Arm's-length employees</dt><dd><div id="ajax-def-arm"><p>For most small businesses, workers are usually <strong>arm's-length </strong>employees unless they are an owner or an owner's family member.</p> </div></dd>
<dt>Non-arm's-length employees</dt><dd><div id="ajax-def-nonarm"><p>In a small business, a <strong>non-arm's-length </strong>employee would most often be:</p>

<ul>
<li>a business owner </li>
<li>a member of an owner's immediate family (for example, a spouse, parent, child, sibling, grandparent, or grandchild) </li>
</ul>
<p>It's possible (but less common) for a non-arm's-length employee relationship to exist between unrelated people, depending on the circumstances.</p> </div></dd>

<p>Learn more if you're not sure about whether an employee is considered arm's length or non-arm's length, or have a business involving more complex control (like a corporation, trust, or partnership): <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/technical-information/income-tax/income-tax-folios-index/series-1-individuals/folio-5-transfer-income-property-rights-third-parties/income-tax-folio-s1-f5-c1-related-persons-dealing-arms-length.html">Income Tax Folio S1-F5-C1, Related persons and dealing at arm's length</a>.</p>
