Board, lodging, housing and utilities expenses

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide your employee, including the superindendent of an apartment block, with a house, apartment or similar accommodation rent free or for less that fair market value. You may also pay or reimburse the utilities expenses of your employee.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit you provide to your employee is considered housing and utilities expenses or board and lodging

    You need to determine if the benefit you provide to your employee is considered housing and utilities expenses or board and lodging. For example:

    • If the benefit is board and lodging, do not continue to next step.

      Learn more: Board and lodging

    • If the benefit is housing and utilities expenses, continue to Step 2 - Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit.

  2. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit

    The housing or utilities expenses you provide to your employees may not be taxable if the benefit is in one of the following situations:

    • Your employee works in a remote location
    • Your employee works at a special work site, including a work site located in a prescribed zone
    • Your employee is a member of the clergy or of a religious order

    • If the benefit is in one of the above situation, do not continue to next step.

      Learn more on the above exceptions using the links.

    • If the benefit is not in one of the above situations, continue to Step 3 - Determine if a benefit is taxable.

  3. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    The benefit is taxable of one of the following apply:

    • You provide an employee, including the superintendent of an apartment block, with a house, apartment or similar accommodation rent free or for less than the FMV of the accommodations
    • You reimburse or provide an allowance to your employee for utilities expenses
    • If the benefit is not for housing, continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit.
    • If the benefit is for housing, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit.

  4. Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit

    If the benefit is for housing, your employee is eligible for a reduction for the value of the benefit in the following order:

    1. Suitability of size

      You provided or paid for the cost of accommodation which is larger than the employee's needs.

      You can reduce the value of accommodation that is appropriate to the employee's needs.

    2. Loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment

      You provided or paid for the cost of accommodation which contains elements that infringe on the employee's privacy or quite enjoyment of the accommodation.

      You can reduce the value of the accommodation that reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects the employee.

  5. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

    Board, lodging or housing expenses

    What is the fair market value (FMV)

    The FMV is the highest price that can be obtained on an open market for a property. This is where the buyer and seller consent to the transaction, are well-informed and are dealing at arm's length.

    The FMV of property or a service does not include GST/HST.

    • Fair market value (FMV) of the board, lodging and housing expense per month
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3a)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 3b)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip for board, lodging and housing in code 30 and in box 14

    Utilities expenses

    • Cost of utilities per month
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip for utilities in code 40 and in box 14

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 1 - Calculations

    Fred works at Barney Investments in the city in Ontario, where he lives in an apartment provided by his employer for 6 months. With the help of the Evaluations Division, you determined that the FMV for Fred's rent is $750 per month. The employer also covers the cost of utilities, which is approximately $150 per month. Each month, Fred pays his employer $500 for the apartment.

    Board, lodging or housing expenses

    • $750 is the FMV for Fred's rent per month
    • minus $500 because Fred reimburse his employer for the rent
    • equals $250 is the value of the benefit to be included on Fred's T4 slip for board, lodging and housing in code 30 and in box 14

    Utilities expenses

    • $150 is the cost of utilities per month
    • minus $0 because Fred does not reimburse his employer for the utilities
    • equals $150 is the value of the benefit to be included on Fred's T4 slip for utilities in code 40 and in box 14
    Example 2 - Calculations

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a two-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,200 per month.

    Board, lodging or housing expenses

    • $1,200 is the FMV for Tim's rent per month
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the rent
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit to be included on Fred's T4 slip for board, lodging and housing in code 30 and in box 14
    Example 3 - Calculations (reduction of the value, step 3)

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a three-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,800 per month. The employer determined, based on the FMV of an apartment or house with two bedrooms, that only $1,200 is appropriate.

    Board, lodging or housing expenses

    • $1,800 is the total value of the rent per month
    • minus $600 is the value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3b)
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit to be included on Fred's T4 slip for board, lodging and housing in code 30 and in box 14

  6. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  7. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities

    Learn how to report the benefit on a slip: Fill out the slips and summaries - File information returns (slips and summaries)
