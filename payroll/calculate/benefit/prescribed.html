Travel, housing and utilities expenses - Provided to an employee in prescribed zones

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit for travel, board, lodging, housing and utilities expenses to your employee who works or lives in a location that is in a prescribed zone in connection with or in the course of their office or employment duties.

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the duties performed are considered temporary

    • If the duties performed are not considered temporary, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the duties performed are considered temporary, continue to: Step 2 - Determine if the principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE)

  2. Determine if the principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE)

    • If the principal place of residence is not considered a SCDE, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the principal place of residence is considered a SCDE, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if the distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable

  3. Determine if the distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable

    • If the distance is considered reasonable, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the distance is considered unreasonable, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours

  4. Determine if your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours

    • If your employee was do not required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours, continue to: Step 5 - Fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site before you exclude board, loding and transportation expenses

  5. Fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site before you exclude travel, housing or utilities expenses

    • If you and your employee did not fill out Form TD4, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If you and your employee filled out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site, continue to: Step 6 - Determine if the benefit is considered paid in a prescribed zone

  6. Determine if the benefit is considered paid in a prescribed zone

    Select one of the following province or territory to determine if the special work site is located in a prescribed zone:

    • Alberta
    • British Columbia
    • Manitoba
    • Newfoundland and Labrador
    • Nova Scotia
    • Ontario
    • Quebec
    • Saskatchewan
    • Yukon, Nunavut, and the Northwest Territories
    • Do not continue to next step if the special work site is not located in a prescribed zone. Refer to: Travel, housing and utilities expenses - Provided to an employee in special work sites
    • If the special work site is located in a prescribed zone, continue to Step 8 - Determine if a benefit is taxable

  7. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    Generally, travel, housing and utilities expenses you provide or reimburse to your employees are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you paid an allowance or reimburse your employee, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • The duties performed are considered temporary (step 1)
    • The principal place of residence is considered a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE) (step 2)
    • The distance between the principal place of residence and the special work site is considered unreasonable (step 3)
    • Your employee was required by employment duties to be away for at least 36 hours (step 4)
    • Form TD4 is completed (step 5)
    • Benefit or allowance is in a location that is considered a prescribed zone (step 6)
    • Allowance is reasonable
    • If the benefit is for travel expenses, it was for a period during which the employee also received housing and utilities benefits
    • If the benefit is for travel expenses, it must relate to travel between the work location and the employee's principal place of residence
    • If the benefit is for travel expenses, it must relate to travel between the work location and a location in Canada or in the country in which the employee is working
    • If the benefit is for medical travel expenses, it must paid or reimbursed under the terms of a private health services plan
    • If the benefit is for medical travel expenses, the expenses must be eligible for the medical expense tax credit (METC)

    Taxable situation

    If you provide the following benefits to your employee, it is taxable:

    • For both business and personal travel, the personal portion is taxable
    • For travel expenses that does not meet all of the conditions above, the full amount is taxable
    • Free or subsidized board and lodging
    • Allowance or you reimburse for housing or utilities

    However, your employee may be eligible to a reduction to the amount for housing expenses of the taxable benefit if the benefit meets all conditions.

    • If the benefit is taxable and not related to housing expenses, continue to: Calculate the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
    • If the benefit is for housing expenses, continue to: Step 9 - Determine if the prescribed zone that contains the employee’s place of residence or work have a developed rental market

  9. Determine if the housing expenses reduction amount

    If the benefit is for housing, you can reduce the value of the benefit in the following order:

    1. Suitability of size

      You provided or paid for the cost of housing which is larger than the employee's needs.

      You can reduce the value of housing expense that is appropriate to the employee's needs.

    2. Loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment

      You provided or paid for the cost of housing which contains elements that infringe on the employee's privacy or quite enjoyment of the housing expense.

      You can reduce the value of the housing expense that reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects the employee.

  10. Calculate the value of the benefit

    In places in prescribed zones without developed rental markets, you have to use other methods to set a value on the housing benefit. The method you use depends on whether you own the residence or rent it from a third party.

    If you provide both rent and utilities and can calculate their cost as separate items, you have to determine their value separately. Add both items to get the value of the housing benefit.

    If your employee reimburses you for all or part of their rent or utilities, determine the benefit as explained below. Subtract any amount reimbursed by your employee and include the amount that remains in their income.

    Ceiling amounts for housing benefits

    The ceiling amount for housing benefits is the maximum amount you can include in an employee's income if the employee does not pay any expenses related to housing or utilities.

    The following table shows the monthly ceiling for housing benefits (including GST/HST) for the years 2022 and 2023, by type of housing and utilities.

    Monthly ceiling for housing benefits
    Type of housing Description of services 2023 2022
    Common shelter N/A $227 $213
    Apartment and duplex Rent only $612 $575
    Utilities only $297 $279
    Rent and utilities $910 $855
    House and trailer Rent only $1,024 $962
    Utilities only $452 $425
    Rent and utilities $1,476 $1,387
    Calculate: Prescribed zones that has developed rental markets

    If there is an established housing market in the prescribed zone where the employee lives, the value of the benefit related to the housing or utilities you provide the employee corresponds to the fair market value (FMV) of the housing or the FMV of the public utilities, including the GST/HST.

    Calculate: Accommodations you own - Prescribed zones that do not have developed rental markets

    If you own a residence that you provide rent free to your employee, report as a benefit whichever of the following amounts is less:

    • Fair market value of the rent
    • Ceiling amount

    Similarly, the amount you have to report as a benefit for utilities is whichever of the following amounts is less:

    • Fair market value of the utilities
    • Ceiling amount
    Calculate: Accommodations you rent from a third party - Prescribed zones that do not have developed rental markets

    If you rent a residence from a third party and provide it rent free to your employee, report as a benefit whichever of the following amounts is less:

    • Amount you pay the third party
    • Ceiling amount

    Similarly, the amount you have to report as a benefit for utilities is whichever of the following amounts is less:

    • Amount you pay the third party
    • Ceiling amount

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the travel, housing or ustility expenses
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 3)
    • minus Amount of the travel assistance that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals Value of the travel assistance that is taxable
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Amount of the travel assistance that is taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in boxes 14 and 32)
    Example 1 - Travel assistance calculations

    Employer paid an employee $2,000 for them to take a trip from the special work site to their principal place of residence and back in Ontario. Employer paid an employee $850 to assist them to make a personal shopping trip from the special work site in a prescribed zone to a different nearby community on a different trip. The special work site was located 27 kilometres from a town with a population of 43,000 people. Employer and employee completed Form TD4, Declaration of Exemption – Employment at a Special Work Site

    • $2,850 ($2,000 + $850) is the total value of the travel assistance
    • minus $2,000 is the amount of the travel assistance that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals $850 is the value of the travel assistance that is taxable
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $850 is the amount of the travel assistance that is taxable to be included the employee's T4 slip (box 14 and box 32)

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 2 - Housing and utilities calculations

    An employer provides an employee with board and lodging in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market with a fair market value of $4,000. The accommodations were owned by the employer. The ceiling value of the board and lodging was $3,000.

    • $4,000 is the total value of the board and lodging
    • minus $1,000 is the amount for the difference between the fair market value and the cleiling amount that is not taxable (to be included on the employee's T4 slip in box 31)
    • equals $3,000 is the value of the board and lodging that is taxable
    • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
    • equals $3,000 is the amount of the board and lodging that is taxable to be included the employee's T4 slip (box 14 and box 32)

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

      • Code 31 - Special work site (exempt portion of the housing)
      • Code 32 - Travel in a prescribed zone (total amount of benefit)
      • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
      • Code 40 - Other Information, utilities

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, housing
      • Code 31 - Special work site (exempt portion of the housing)
      • Code 32 - Travel in a prescribed zone (total amount of benefit)
      • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
      • Code 40 - Other Information, utilities
    T4A slip - Information for payers

    Report on:

    • Code 28 - Other income (total amount of travel benefit)
    • Code 116 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (medical portion only)
    • Code 124 - Board and lodging at special work sites (exempt portion)
