What is a shareholder benefit

You may need to determine if a person received a shareholder benefit for the purposes of subsection 15(1) of the Income Tax Act.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Confirm if this content applies to you

    This content only applies to persons who are one of the following:

    • Shareholders
    • are members of a partnership that is a shareholder
    • are contemplated shareholders
    • who are not at arm's length with a shareholder of the corporation that gave them a benefit

    It also only applies to persons who received a benefit and had knowledge they received a benefit before there was an issue with the CRA.

    The content may not apply to situations where the person is deemed to have received a shareholder benefit, including a benefit that is:

    • An acquisition of shares from a division of a non-resident corporation
    • A corporate loan to a shareholder or connected person
    • A corporate loan to an individual due to employment or services
    • A directed to a third party by the taxpayer

    The content also may not apply to exceptions under subsection 15(1), which includes benefits that are:

    • Dividends or stock dividend
    • Rights to purchase shares that are identical to the rights given to all other shareholders
    • Given by the reduction of the paid-up capital of the corporation
    • Given by the redemption, acquisition, or cancellation of shares
    • Given on the winding-up or reorganization of the corporation
