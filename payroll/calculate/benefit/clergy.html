Travel, board and lodging expenses - Provided to a member of the clergy

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit or an allowance to your employee who is a member of the clergy to compensate for the cost of travel expenses (other than automobile or motor vehicle expenses), board or lodging in connection with or in the course of their office or employment duties.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if the traveller is considered in a special situation

    The benefit may not be taxable if the travel expenses you provide to your employee is in one of the following special situations:

    • Refer to: Your employee is working in a prescribed zone
    • Refer to: Your employee works in a remote location
    • Refer to: Your employee works at a special work site
    • Refer to: Your employee has a severe or prolonged impairment
    • If the travel expenses benefit is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the travel expenses benefit is not in one of the above special situations, the benefit provided for travel expenses may be a taxable benefit. Continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable.

  2. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    You need to determine if the benefit is taxable based on the type of expense you provided to the member of the clergy.

    1. Travel expenses

      Non-taxable situation

      Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, if you paid an allowance or reimburse your employee who is a member of the clergy for travel expenses, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

      • Employee is required to travel in the course of their employment duties
      • Travel allowance is reasonable, for meal expenses this is generally up to $23 including taxes (higher amounts may be reasonable if the meal cost in a location is higher)
      • You can justify your position regarding the amount being reasonable

      Taxable situation

      If the allowance or reimbursement you provide to your employee for travel expenses do not meet all of the conditions above, the full amount is a taxable benefit.

      • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations. Do not continue to next step.
      • If the benefit is taxable, continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip

    2. Board and lodging expenses

      Taxable situation

      If you provide an allowance or you reimburse your employee who is a member of the clergy for board and lodging expenses, the benefit is taxable.

      However, your employee may be eligible to a reduction to the amount if the benefit meets certain conditions.

      Continue to: Step 3 - Determine if your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction

  3. Determine if your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction

    Your employee may be eligible to claim a clergy residence deduction in respect of their residence when calculating the employment income if both of the following apply:

    • Your employee's status is one of the following:
      • Member of the clergy
      • Member of a religious order
      • Regular minister of a religious denomination
    • Your employee's function is one of the following:
      • Ministering to a diocese, parish or congregation
      • Engaged exclusively in full-time administrative service by appointment of a religious order or religious denomination

    Learn more: IT141R- Clergy Residence Deduction

    • If the employee is not eligible, continue to: Step 5 - Calculate the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip.
    • If the employee is eligible, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if the board or lodging are eligible for the reduction amount

  4. Determine the limit of the deduction to calculate the value of the benefit

    The amount of the deduction depends upon whether the living accommodation the clergy member occupy is:

    • Supplied to them by virtue of their employment
    • Rented by them
    • Owned by them

    Although the clergy residence deduction and the utilities share of the benefit can be excluded from income for the purpose of calculating tax deductions and CPP, you still have to report them on the T4 slip.

    The deduction cannot be more than the amount of the employee's income for the year from the qualifying office or employment.

    Situations
    Situation: You provide free or low-rent housing (benefit)

    If you provide free or low-rent housing, your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction if all of the following apply:

    • In respect to rent and utilities paid or the fair market value of the residence that is paid on behalf of the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
    • Reduction reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects your employee

    To help you calculate the deduction, the clergy member must provide one of the following:

    • Completed Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, do not need the form to CRA
    • Letter indicating their member's intention to claim the deduction, including the amount of the deduction that will be claimed
    5. Situation: You provide an allowance to the clergy member who rents or owns their residence

    If you provide an allowance for the cost incurred to the clergy that own their own house or rent a property, your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction if all of the following apply:

    • In respect to rent and utilities paid or the fair market value of the residence that is owned and occupied by the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
    • Reduction reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects your employee

    To help you calculate the deduction, the clergy member must do all of the following:

    • Provides you with a completed Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction
    • Provides you with a completed Form T1213, Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source and they provide you with the Letter of Authority that is approved by a tax services office
    • Send Forms T1213 and T1223 to the address provided on the T1213 form
    • Provides you with the Letter of Authority from the CRA that is approved by a tax services office

    Learn more: Employee wants to reduce income tax deductions

  5. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the housing or utilities expenses
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of clergy residence (step 3a)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3b)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 3b)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
    Example 1 - Calculations

    Fred works at Barney Investments in the city in Ontario, where he lives in an apartment provided by his employer for 6 months. With the help of the Evaluations Division, you determined that the FMV for Fred's rent is $750 per month. The employer also covers the cost of utilities, which is approximately $150 per month. Each month, Fred pays his employer $500 for the apartment.

    • $900 ($750 + $150) is the total value of the housing or utilities that are taxable per month
    • minus $500 because Fred reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $400 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Fred's T4 slip are $400 in box 14, $250 in code 30 and $150 in code 40

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 2 - Calculations

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a two-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,200 per month.

    • $1,200 is the total value of the housing that is taxable per month
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Tim's T4 slip are $1,200 in box 14 and $1,200 in code 30

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 3 - Calculations (reduction of the value)

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a three-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,800 per month. The employer determined, based on the FMV of an apartment or house with two bedrooms, that only $1,200 is appropriate.

    • $1,800 is the total value of the housing that is taxable per month
    • minus $600 ($1,800 - $1,200) is the value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3b)
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Tim's T4 slip are $1,200 in box 14 and $1,200 in code 30

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  7. Report the benefit on a slip
