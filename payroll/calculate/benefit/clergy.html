Board, lodging, housing and utilities expenses - Provided to a member of the clergy

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit or an allowance to your employee who is a member of the clergy to compensate for the cost of board, lodging, housing or utilities in connection with or in the course of their office or employment duties.

On this page

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if your employee is considered in a special situation

    The benefit may not be taxable if your employee is in one of the following special situations:

    • Refer to: Your employee is working in a prescribed zone
    • Refer to: Your employee works in a remote location
    • Refer to: Your employee works at a special work site
    • Refer to: Your employee has a severe or prolonged impairment
    • If the benefit is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is not in one of the above special situations, the benefit may be taxable. Continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable.

  2. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    Board, lodging, housing or utilities expenses

    Taxable situation

    If you provide the following benefits to your employee, it is taxable:

    • Free or subsidized board and lodging
    • Allowance or you reimburse for housing or utilities

    However, your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order may be eligible for a reduction to the amount taxable if the benefit meets all conditions.

    Other benefits

    If you provide an allowance or you reimburse your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order for other benefits, the same tax treatment that apply to most employees also apply them.

    • If the benefit is not related to board, lodging, housing or utilities expenses, do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If the benefit is for board, lodging, housing or utilities expenses, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or a religous order

  3. Determine if your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or a religous order

    Your employee meets the status and the function conditions if both of the following apply:

    • Your employee's status is one of the following:
      • Member of the clergy
      • Member of a religious order
      • Regular minister of a religious denomination
    • Your employee's function is one of the following:
      • Ministering to a diocese, parish or congregation
      • Engaged exclusively in full-time administrative service by appointment of a religious order or religious denomination

    If your employee meets both conditions, they may be eligible to claim a clergy residence deduction when calculating the value of the benefit and the employment income.

    Learn more: IT141R- Clergy Residence Deduction

    • If your employee does not meet both conditions, your employee is not considered a member of the clergy or a religious order. Do not continue to next step. Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable
    • If your employee meets both conditions, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if your employee is eligible for the clergy residence deduction

  4. Determine if your employee is eligible for the clergy residence deduction

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 3, your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction if all of the following apply:

    • The benefit is in respect to rent and utilities paid or the fair market value of the residence that:

      • is paid on behalf of the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
      • is owned and occupied by the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
    • Your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or religous order (step 3)
    • The reduction reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects your employee

    Although the clergy residence deduction and the utilities share of the benefit can be excluded from income for the purpose of calculating tax deductions and CPP, you still have to report them on the T4 slip.

    • If your employee is not eligible for the reduction, continue to: Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit
    • If your employee meets all conditions to be eligible for the reduction, continue to: Step 5 - Get the required documents before you apply the clergy residence reduction

  5. Get the required documents before you apply the clergy residence reduction

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 4, your employee must provide you with documents before you can apply the reduction.

    Situation: You are providing free or low-rent housing (benefits)

    If you are providing free or low-rent housing (benefits), your employee must provide one of the following:

    • A completed Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, do not send the form to CRA.
    • A letter indicating their member's intention to claim the deduction, including the amount of the deduction that will be claimed.

    You can reduce the income by the amount indicated on line 11 of the Form or the amount indicated on the letter.

    Situation: Your employee owns or rents their residence (allowance)

    If your employee owns or rents their residence (allowance), your employee must do all of the following:

    • Complete Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction
    • Complete Form T1213, Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source
    • Send Forms T1213 and T1223 to the CRA at the address provided on Form T1213
    • Provide you with the CRA Letter of Authority.

    You can only reduce the income by the amount indicated in the letter.

    Examples

    Example 1: Income tax

    When the clergy member lives in their own house or rents a property and claims a clergy residence deduction based on the fair market value of the house or the rent paid, respectively, they will need to file a T1213, or written letter, with the local tax service office.

    When the request is approved, the employer will reduce the employee's taxable income by the amount of the clergy residence deduction and withhold income tax at source on the difference.

    Example 2: Income tax

    When the clergy member lives in employer-provided accommodations and claims the clergy residence deduction, the deduction will generally be offset by the income inclusion relating to the taxable benefit of employer-provided accommodations.

    In this scenario, there is no change in income for withholding tax purposes, and a T1213 or written request, is not required to be filed.

    When determining the amount of income subject to withholding tax, the employer should include the taxable benefit relating to employer provided accommodations in the employee's income.

    The employee's taxable income should then be reduced by the clergy residence deduction which the employee will be claiming.

    Example 3: CPP

    When calculating the amount of CPP to withhold, the employer should reduce the employee's earnings by the amount of the clergy residence deduction and withhold CPP contributions on the net amount.

    In this situation, the employees must inform their employer, in writing, of their intent to claim the clergy residence deduction (this may include Form T1223).

    If the employee also wants a reduction of income tax at source, as noted above, they must request a waiver by completing and filing Form T1213 with their local tax service office.

    Example 4: CPP

    When calculating pensionable earnings, the clergy residence deduction will generally be offset by the income inclusion relating to the taxable benefit of employer provided accommodations.

    As the deduction and income inclusion generally net out, there are no changes required to the clergy member's income for the purpose of withholding CPP at source.

    In this situation, the employees must inform their employer, in writing, of their intent to claim the clergy residence deduction (this may include Form T1223). However, they need not file a T1213.

    • If your employee does not provide the documents, do not apply the reduction.
    • If your employee provides you with the documents, you can apply the clergy residence reduction.

  6. Calculate the value of the benefit

    The value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the benefit
    • minus Amount eligible for the clergy reduction amount (step 5)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    The deduction cannot be more than the amount of the employee's income for the year from the qualifying office or employment.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  7. Determine if your employee who is a member of a religious order has taken a vow of perpetual poverty

    You need to determine if your employee decided to take a vow of perpetual poverty. This is a pledge or promise of a person to give their income to the religious order of which they are a member.

    You do not withhold payroll deductions. However, you must report the income on the T4 slip on Box 14 – Employment income, the benefits (if applicable), and both CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt (Box 28).

    Learn more: IT86R- Vow of perpetual poverty

    • If the employee has taken a vow of perpetual property, do not withhold payroll deductions, continue to: Step 8 - Report the benefit on a slip.
    • If the employee has not taken a vow of perpetual property, continue to: Step 7 - Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  8. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  9. Report the benefit on a slip

    You must report the following on the T4 slip.

    Report - Most employees without a vow of perpetual poverty

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, eligible utilities for Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, non-eligible utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, eligible utilities for Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, non-eligible utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction
    Report - Employees with a vow of perpetual poverty (step 6)

    You must report the following amounts on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 28 - CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt
      • Code 30 - Other Information, eligible utilities for Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, non-eligible utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 28 - CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt
      • Code 30 - Other Information, eligible utilities for Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, non-eligible utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction
    • Use code 30, Housing, board, and lodging, if the employer provided an employee with free or subsidized housing (accommodation) even if the employee is claiming a clergy residence deduction. This includes eligible utilities (electricity, heat, water, sewer). Report all other utilities under code 40.
    • Use code 40, Other taxable allowances and benefits, to report any allowances paid by the employer in respect of the accommodations and unreasonable travel and transportation allowances.
    • Use code 40, Other taxable allowances and benefits, in a separate box, to report utilities benefits in situations where the employee has not notified the employer of their intention to claim a deduction for the residence and the employer has paid the utilities.
