Determine if the benefit is taxable

Generally, the benefits received under or out of employee benefit plans are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act.

Non-taxable situation

If the benefit is derived from an employee benefit plan, the benefit is not taxable if one of the following apply:

  • The benefit is a death benefit or an amount that would be a death benefit but for the deduction provided in the definition of the term in subsection 248(1)
    • The benefit is a return of amounts contributed to the plan by the person or a deceased employee of whom the person is an heir or legal representative
    • The amounts in question was not deducted from the person’s or your employee’s income for any taxation year
    • The benefit is a superannuation or pension
    • It is for services rendered in a period throughout which your employee was not resident in Canada

If the benefit is a designated employee benefit (for example: a group sickness or accident insurance plan, a group term life insurance plan, or a private health services plan, continue to: Insurance plans.

Taxable situation

If the benefit does not meet all the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

Continue to Calculate the value of the benefit.

Calculate the value of the benefit

If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to:

  • Fair market value of the benefit received or enjoyed
  • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
  • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip

Report the benefit on a slip

You must report the following amounts on the slip:

T4 slip - Current employees

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other information
    • Code 85 - Other information, Employee-paid premiums for private health services plans optional

  • Cash: Option 2

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other information
    • Code 85 - Other information, Employee-paid premiums for private health services plans optional
Report: T4A slip - Former or retired employees
  • Code 028 - Other information, Other income - Income maintenance, sickness, accident, or disability insurance plans
  • Code 118 - Other information, Medical premium benefits - Provincial hospitalization or medical care insurance premiums
  • Code 119 - Other information, Premiums paid to a group term life insurance plan
  • Code 135 - Other information, Recipient-paid premiums for private health services plans optional
