Board, lodging, housing and utilities expenses

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide a benefit or an allowance to your employee to compensate for the cost of board, lodging, housing or utilities in connection with or in the course of their office or employment duties.

  • Steps
  • References

Steps

  1. Determine if your employee is considered in a special situation

    The benefit may not be taxable if your employee is in one of the following special situations:

    • Refer to: Your employee is working in a prescribed zone
    • Refer to: Your employee works in a remote location
    • Refer to: Your employee works at a special work site
    • Refer to: Your employee has a severe or prolonged impairment
    • Refer to: Your employee is a member of the clergy
    • If the benefit is in one of the above special situation, do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is not in one of the above special situations, the benefit may be taxable. Continue to Step 2 - Determine if the benefit is taxable.

  2. Determine if a benefit is taxable

    Generally, board, loding, housing and utilities you provide or pay on behalf of your employees are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act or the CRA's administrative policy .

    Non-taxable situation

    Under the Income Tax Act (ITA), if you paid an allowance or reimburse your employee who is a participant or member of a sports team or recreational program for board and lodging expenses, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

    • Amount is equal or less then $386 (for 2022) per month
    • You are a registered charity or a non-profit organization
    • Participation or membership with the team or program is restricted to persons under 21 years of age
    • Allowance is for a participant or member who has to live away from their ordinary place of residence
    • Allowance is not attributable to any services, such as coaching or refereeing, that your employee provides to the team or program

    Taxable situation

    If you provide the following benefits to your employee, it is taxable:

    • Free or subsidized board and lodging
    • Allowance or you reimburse for housing or utilities

    However, your employee may be eligible for a reduction to the amount taxable if the benefit meets all conditions.

    • If the benefit is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations. Do not continue to next step.
    • If the benefit is taxable, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if your employee is eligible for the reduction

  3. Determine if your employee is eligible for the reduction

    You need to determine if you can reduce the value of the benefit for the housing or utilities.

    The two factors that can reduce the value of the benefit applies in the following order:

    1. Suitability of size - You provide or pay for the cost of housing which is larger than the employee's needs. You can reduce the value of accommodation that is appropriate to the employee's needs.
    2. Loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment - You provide or pay for the cost of housing which contains elements that infringe on the employee's privacy or quite enjoyment of the accommodation. You can reduce the value of the accommodation that reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects the employee.

  4. Calculate the value of the benefit

    If the benefit is taxable, the value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    • Total value of the housing or utilities expenses
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3)
    • minus Value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment (step 3)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip
    Example 1 - Calculations

    Fred works at Barney Investments in the city in Ontario, where he lives in an apartment provided by his employer for 6 months. With the help of the Evaluations Division, you determined that the FMV for Fred's rent is $750 per month. The employer also covers the cost of utilities, which is approximately $150 per month. Each month, Fred pays his employer $500 for the apartment.

    • $900 ($750 + $150) is the total value of the housing or utilities that are taxable per month
    • minus $500 because Fred reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $400 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Fred's T4 slip are $400 in box 14, $250 in code 30 and $150 in code 40

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 2 - Calculations

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a two-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,200 per month.

    • $1,200 is the total value of the housing that is taxable per month
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Tim's T4 slip are $1,200 in box 14 and $1,200 in code 30

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 3 - Calculations (reduction of the value)

    Tim works as a superintendent in a large apartment building. His employer provides him with a three-bedroom apartment at no charge for 6 months, which he usually rents out for $1,800 per month. The employer determined, based on the FMV of an apartment or house with two bedrooms, that only $1,200 is appropriate.

    • $1,800 is the total value of the housing that is taxable per month
    • minus $600 ($1,800 - $1,200) is the value eligible for the reduction amount in respect of suitability of size (step 3b)
    • minus $0 because Tim does not reimburse his employer for the housing
    • equals $1,200 is the value of the benefit per month, the amounts to be included on Tim's T4 slip are $1,200 in box 14 and $1,200 in code 30

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  5. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  6. Report the benefit on a slip

    If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, Utilities

    • Cash: Option 2

      Report on:

      • Box 14 - Employment Income
      • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
      • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
      • Code 30 - Other Information, Board and lodging
      • Code 40 - Other Information, Utilities
