Educational assistance

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

You may provide educational assistance to your employee, a former employee or an employee's family member. For example:

  • Reimbursement or payment of tuition and training fees
  • Scholarships and bursaries
  • Subsidized school services
  • Educational allowances

It important to make the difference between scholarships and bursaries, and a research grant.

What is a scholarship

Scholarships are amounts awarded to students, or on their behalf, to enable them to pursue their education. They can include amounts paid to defray living expenses, as well as amounts that are directly related to tuition fees. They usually apply to studies at the postsecondary level or beyond, intended to obtain a university degree, a diploma or a certificate. However, there are circumstances under which scholarships are awarded for education below the postsecondary school level. Normally, a student is not expected to do specific work for the payer in exchange for a scholarship or bursary.

What is a bursary

Bursaries are similar to scholarships in the sense that they are amounts or benefits awarded to individuals to enable them to pursue their education. However, the recipient is usually a graduate student and the payer is a university, charity or similar organization. In general, bursaries are awarded for doctoral studies or postdoctoral work.

What is a research grant

Research Grants are a sum of money provided to an individual for the payment of expenses necessary to conduct a research project. The grant may include personal or living expenses. The grant may also include an element of remuneration to the recipient. Funds originating from various sources may be paid to and administered by a college, university, hospital, or similar institution where the researcher is employed. However, only that part of the grant paid to, or otherwise available for, the researcher is considered to be a research grant.

Learn more on the different types of research grants: Research Grants in Canada

Determine if the benefit is taxable

Generally, if you pay, reimburse or provide an allowance for educational expenses to your employee, a former employee or an employee's family member, the benefit is taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act or the CRA's administrative policy .

Situations

Situation: You provide or reimburse for tuition and training fees to your current or former employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act, if you provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your current or former employee, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following applies:

  • Training is specific or general employment-related
  • Your employee returns to work for you for a reasonable amount of time (you must be able to justify your position regarding the length of the period of time being reasonable)
  • Training is not for your employee's personal interest

You must be able to clearly demonstrate you are the primary beneficiary of the expense.

What is a specific employment-related, general employment-related or personal interest training

The CRA considered following 3 categories of training:

  • Specific employment-related training

    Training taken to maintain or upgrade employment-related skills are mainly for the employer's benefit when it is reasonable to assume that the employee will resume their employment for a reasonable period of time after they finish the course.

    Tuition fees and other associated costs such as books, meals, travel, and accommodation that employers pay for courses leading to a degree, diploma, or certificate in a field related to the employee's current or future responsibilities in the employer's business.

  • General employment-related training

    Training for general employment-related, even if not directly related to an employer's business, are taken mainly for the employer's benefit.

    For example, fees the employer pays for stress management, employment equity, first aid, and language courses.

  • Personal interest training

    Training for personal interest or technical skills not related to the employer's business are taken mainly for the employee's benefit.

Your employee cannot claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit, or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Taxable situation

If the benefit for educational assistance does not meet all of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

Situation: You provide or reimburse for tuition and training fees to your future employee

Non-taxable situation

If you provide or reimburse for a course, a program, or training to your future employee, this is not taxable as an employee benefit because there is no employee/employer relationship between the payer and the future employee (student).

The individual must report the amounts received on their T1 return.

Example

A student may receive educational assistance in exchange for which they agree to work at the end of their studies or training for the person granting the financial assistance.

Report on T4A slip

The payer must report the full amount of the educational assistance paid on a T4A slip. If they meet certain criteria, the individual may claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Taxable situation

If the benefit does not meet all of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

Situation: You provide or reimburse for certain educational assistance to your employee's family member

You may provide or reimburse for certain education assistance to your employees' family member who is at school or university.

Situations

Situation: You provide free or reduced tuition fees to your employee’s family member

Non-taxable situation

If you provide free or reduced tuition fees to your employee’s family member, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

  • You and your employee deal at arm’s length
  • Benefit is not provided as a substitute for salary, wages or other remuneration

In this situation, there is no employee/employer relationship between the payer and the family member of the employee. The individual must report the amounts received on their T1 return.

Report on T4A slip

The payer must report the full amount of the educational assistance paid on a T4A slip. If they meet certain criteria, the individual may claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Continue to Report the benefit on a slip.

Taxable situation

If the benefit does not meet all of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

Continue to Calculate the value of the benefit.

Situation: You pay or reimburse your employee for educational allowance for their children

Non-taxable situation

If you pay or reimburse your employee for educational allowance for their children, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

  • Employee and their family has to live in a specific location away from their home
  • Schools in the area do not meet the educational needs of your employee’s children
  • Education is provided in the official language of Canada primarily used by your employee
  • Child is in full-time attendance at the school
  • Subsidy you provide is reasonable

In this situation, there is no employee/employer relationship between the payer and the family member of the employee. The individual must report the amounts received on their T1 return.

Report on T4A slip

The payer must report the full amount of the educational allowance paid on a T4A slip. If they meet certain criteria, the individual may claim the non-refundable tuition tax credit or the educational amount, on their T1 return.

Situation: You provide free or subsidized school services to your employee’s children (such as running a boarding school that the employee's child or children attend)

Special situations

If you provide free or subsidized school services to your employee’s children (such as running a boarding school that the employee's child or children attend), do not continue if in one of the following situation:

  • Employment is in a remote work location, refer to Board and lodging provided in special work sites or remote locations
  • Travel to or from your employee’s place of business, refer to Travel expenses provided in special work sites or remote locations

Taxable situation

If the benefit is not in the situations above, the benefit is taxable.

T4 slip - Employers
T4A slip - Payers

If the benefit is not taxable, you must report the following amounts on the T4A slip.

  • Do not withhold CPP, EI or income tax deductions
  • If the payment is for research grants, report the payments on a T4A slip in Code 104 - Other information
  • If the payment is for scholarships, fellowships, bursaries, and artists' project grants, report the payments on a T4A slip in Code 105 - Other information
  • Do not include GST/HST and PST in the amount reported in codes 104 or 105

Under the CRA administrative policy, a slip must be issued if the total of all payments in the calendar year were more than $500.
