Payments to First Nations workers

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement or to the CRA administrative policy.

You must report all income and deductions on a T4 or T4A slip for First Nations wokers regardless of whether the income is taxable or exempt.

Many Indigenous people in Canada prefer not to describe themselves as "Indians" and view this term as rooted in colonialism and racism. Under the Indian Act, the precise legal meaning of the term "Indian" refers to First Nations persons who are entitled to registration.

Steps

  1. Determine if the worker is registered with Indigenous Services Canada (ISC)

    You must determine if the worker is registered with ISC and their effective date for registration.

    The CRA requires proof of registration with ISC. The effective date for registration means the date that the First Nations worker is eligible for the tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act on the income they earn on a reserve.

    ISC is responsible for the Indian Register and is the only one authorized to issue a "certificate of Indian status" to an individual.

    Learn more on how to prove registration from ISC: How to prove registration

    • If the worker does not have proof of registration, do not continue to next step.

      You must deduct CPP, EI and income tax the same as regular workers.

      The exemption under section 87 only applies to a worker who is registered or entitled to be registered under the Indian Act.

    Situation: Your employee lives on a reserve and you are a resident on a reserve

    Exempt income situation

    The status Indian individual's (status confirmed in step 1) income is exempt from income tax under section 87 of the Indian Act if all of the following apply:

    • Worker lives on a reserve
    • Employer is a resident on a reserve

    Where an employee is a shareholder who controls the business (non-arm's length with the corporation), the source of the business income may be relevant in determining whether the tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act applies to the income received in the shareholder's capacity as an employee. Where the source of the business income in this situation is off the reserve, the employment income (salary and bonuses) will not be considered to be connected to the reserve and will not be exempt from tax.

    Situation: Other situations where your employee is required to perform duties on a reserve

    Depending on your situation, the employment income of status Indians may be exempt or partly exempt from tax. It is the income that is exempt from tax and not the person.

    The rules for CPP and EI are different. EI premiums are always required. Income that is exempt from income tax is not pensionable employment although you or the employee may elect into the CPP.

    To determine if the income is exempt or partly from tax in this situation, continue to: Step 3: Determine the percentage of duties related to employment on a reserve.

  4. Determine the percentage of duties related to employment on a reserve

    To determine the portion of employment duties performed on a reserve, two factors are generally taken into account:

    • Whether an actual "duty of employment" was performed (the employee was required to perform the particular task and was compensated by the employer for performing that particular task)
    • The extent to which the duty of employment was performed on a reserve
    How to calculate the percentage of duties related to employment performed on and off a reserve
    • Total working days on a reserve (do not include vacation or sick leave)
    • divide by Total working days
    • equals Result
    • multiply by 100
    • equals This is the % of duties of employment performed on reserve (paid leave such as vacation or sick leave is also exempt)
    Calculation example

    An employer requires their status Indian employee to perform duties related to their employment both on and off reserve. The employee had a total of 220 working days in the year, 44 of which were on reserve. These days spent working on the reserve did not include either vacation or sick leave.

    The percentage of the status Indian employee’s duties related to employment performed on a reserve is calculated as follows:

    • 44 total Working days on a reserve (not including vacation or sick leave)
    • divide by 220 total working days
    • equals 0.2 is the result
    • multiply by 100
    • equals 20% is the percentage of duties of employment performed on reserve (paid leave such as vacation or sick leave is also exempt)

      Situation: Your employee performs 50% or less of their employment duties on a reserve

      Partly-exempt income

      The status Indian individual's (status confirmed in step 1) income is partly-exempt from income tax under section 87 of the Indian Act if if the employee performs 50% or less of their employment duties on a reserve. The exemption may be prorated where those duties are not merely incidental to duties they performed off a reserve. The exemption will apply only to the portion of the income related to the duties the employee performed on the reserve if all of the following apply:

      • Employer or the employee is in one of the following situations:

      Exempt income situation

      The status Indian individual's (status confirmed in step 1) income is exempt from income tax under section 87 of the Indian Act if all of the following apply:

      • Employer is one of the following:
        • An Indian band which has a reserve, or a tribal council representing one or more Indian bands that have reserves
        • An Indian organization controlled by an Indian band which has a reserve, or a tribal council representing one or more Indian bands that have reserves, and the organization is dedicated exclusively to the social, cultural, educational, or economic development of Indians who for the most part live on reserves
      • Employee's duties of employment are in connection with the employer's non-commercial activities carried on exclusively for the benefit of Indians who for the most part live on reserves

  5. Withhold payroll deductions and remit the GST/HST

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    You must withhold and remit the deductions depending on the type of worker:

    Worker is an employee

    You must withhold the following deductions:

    • Salary, wages and taxable benefits in cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, on the portion of the income which is taxable
      • CPP, on the portion of the income which is taxable or all income if employer filed a CPT124 election
      • EI

      Remit:

      • GST/HST - Reimbursements
      • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

    • Non-cash and near-cash taxable benefits: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, on the portion of the income which is taxable
      • CPP, on the portion of income which is taxable or all income if employer filed a CPT124 election
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST

    CPP is only withheld on the portion of the income that is taxable. Where the income is not taxable, CPP is not withheld, except if the employer has filed a CPT124 election - Application to Cover the Employment of an Indian in Canada under the Canada Pension Plan whose Income is Exempt under the Income Tax Act.

    If the employer chooses not to apply for coverage, an Indian can elect to pay CPP on tax-exempt earnings on Form CPT20, Election to Pay Canada Pension Plan Contributions when filing their personal income tax return.

  6. Report the payment on a slip

    You must report using the following slip depending on who you made the payment to:

    T4 slip - Payment made to an employee

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 40 - Other Information, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 66 – Eligible retiring allowances
    • Code 69 – Indian (exempt income) – Non-eligible retiring allowances
    • Code 71 – Indian (tax-exempt income) – Employment
    T4 slip - Payment made to a self-employed worker

    Report the self-employment income you paid to a person who is registered, or entitled to be registered under the Indian Act who is a self-employed fisher , a self-employed barber or hairdresser , or a self-employed taxi driver and driver of other passenger-carrying vehicles:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 40 - Other Information, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 78 – Fishers – Gross income, code 79 – Fishers – Net partnership amount and code 80 – Fishers – Shareperson amount for a fisher, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 82 – Taxi drivers and drivers of other passenger-carrying vehicles – Gross income, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 83 – Barbers or hairdressers – Gross income, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 88 – Indian (exempt income) – Self-employment
    T4A slip - Payment made to a person or partnership that was a shareholder

    Report on:

    • Box 016 – Pension or superannuation, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Box 018 – Lump-sum payments, on the portion of the taxable income
    • Code 144 – Indian (exempt income) – Other income
    • Code 146 – Indian (exempt income) – Pension or superannuation
    • Code 148 – Indian (exempt income) – Lump-sum payments
    • Code 195 – Indian (exempt income) – PRPP payments
