Your tax obligations: Employer and employee relationship

Payroll obligations

Generally, any person must open a payroll account (RP), calculate payroll deductions and contributions, remit (pay) those deductions and contributions, and file information returns if they:

  • Pay salaries, wages (including advances), bonuses, vacation pay, or tips to employees
  • Provide certain taxable benefits to employees (for example, an automobile or allowances)

Generally, the employer must withhold and remit the income tax, EI, CPP on payments made related to employment income.

If the employer makes a payment to an employee in an employment relationship, the employer must fill out a T4 slip to report all payments made to that employee in the calendar year.

Learn more: Payroll

Income tax obligations

The employer must report and pay tax on all income earned from taxable activities. The income is the net earnings from employment, property, investments and benefits. To report and pay tax, the employer must calculate the business income and expenses using one of the following forms when filing their income tax return:

  • T1 return (business income)
    • Form T2125, Statement of Business or Professional Activities
    • Form T2042, Statement of Farming Activities
    • Form T2121, Statement of Fishing Activities

    Learn more: Business or professional income

  • T2 return (corporate income)

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: Completing your corporation income tax return

  • T5013 return (partnership income)

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: T5013 Partnership Information Return filing requirements

GST/HST obligations

Generally, any person must open a GST/HST account (RT), collect, file and remit (pay) the GST/HST if they provide goods or services and earn more than $30,000 over 4 calendar quarters.

Learn more: Collect, file, and remit (pay) the GST/HST

Other tax obligations

Employee

Income tax obligations

Employees who are residents of Canada are required to report their worldwide income from all sources.

If the employee is a non-resident, they are subject to Canadian income tax on most Canadian-sourced income paid or credited to them during the year unless all or part of that income is exempt under a tax treaty.

When filing the T1 return, the employee uses the amounts from the T4 slip to report their employment income and source deductions.

Receiving the slip

Generally, the employer must provide the employee with a T4 slip which includes any payment made regardless if the income is taxable or not.

Learn more about the options to receive the information if the employee does not expect to get the slip: Get a copy of a slip
