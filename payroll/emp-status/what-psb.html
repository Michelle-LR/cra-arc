What is a personal services business (PSB)

The CRA is working to help Canadian corporations operating as a personal services business (PSB) and the businesses that hire them to better understand their tax obligations.

Generally, a PSB exists where the person performing the work would be considered to be an employee of the payer if it were not for the existence of the corporation through which they are performing the services.

The CRA might consider your corporation to be a PSB if all 5 conditions for a corporation to be a PSB are met. If they are not met and you provided your services through a corporation, the same tax obligations will apply to you as to any other corporation.

If the person is not performing the services through a corporation, they are not operating a PSB and you should review their employment status to determine your tax obligations.

Payer and payee relationship

If your businesses hires or you operate a PSB, your tax obligations are different from those of an employer and employee.

  • Payer

    The business receiving the services is considered the payer (a person or business who pays the worker or corporation for their services)

  • Payee

    The PSB receiving the payment for those services is considered the payee

Why is it important to determine if the corporation is a PSB

It is important to determine if the corporation is a PSB. Special rules may apply to their corporation for income tax purposes if a worker performs the services on behalf of their own corporation. For example, a PSB is not eligible for the general tax reduction or the small business reduction, which allow other corporations to reduce their corporate tax rates. They are also limited in the expenses they may deduct from their income.

Example of common types of industries

In some industries, such as the following, it is a more common practice for payers to require individuals to incorporate in order to provide services:

  • Real estate, rental and leasing
  • Construction
  • Trucking
  • Transportation and warehousing
  • Professional, scientific, and technical services

In other words, a person who chooses to incorporate their business in order to provide services for one other business might be considered to be operating a PSB.
