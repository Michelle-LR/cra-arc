Determine if the worker is an employee or a self-employed worker

This guidance does not replace the law and does not replace a formal request for a CPP/EI ruling. No changes were made to the existing federal legislative requirements.

You need to consider multiple factors to determine if the worker is an employee or is self-employed. You can use this CRA guidance to help you gather the facts based on your situation.

Steps

  1. Get ready to use this guidance

    Information on the types of employment status for tax purposes, the types of contracts, what to do if you made an error in determining the employment status or what to do if you are not sure if the worker is an employee or self-employed

  2. Factors: Employee or self-employed worker for CPP/EI purposes

    Information on the factors to consider depending on where the employment contract is formed in or where the contract requires the terms to be reviewed according to the laws of the province or territory

  3. Understand the tax obligations

    Information about the withholding and reporting obligations depending on the types of relationships: Employer and employee and payer and self-employed
