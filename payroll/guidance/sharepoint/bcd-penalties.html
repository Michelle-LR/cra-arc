Every person who fails to pay tax instalments by the due date is liable to a penalty equal to 50% of the amount by which the interest payable by the person under section 161 in respect of all instalments for the year exceeds the greater of:

Subsection 162(7.3) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to report an amount

Every person is liable to a penalty who:

  • Fails to report an amount, equal to or greater than $500, required to be included in computing the person's income in a return for a taxation year
  • Had failed to report an amount, equal to or greater than $500, required to be included in computing the person's income in a return filed for any of the three preceding taxation years
  • Is not liable to a penalty under subsection 163(2) in respect of the unreported amount

The amount of the penalty is equal to the lesser of:

  • 10% of the unreported amount
  • The amount determined by the formula

0.5 x (A – B)

where
A is the total of the amounts that would be determined under paragraphs 163(2)(a) to (g) if subsection (2) applied in respect of the unreported amount, and
B is any amount deducted or withheld under subsection 153(1) that may reasonably be considered to be in respect of the unreported amount.

Action

You need to calculate the amount of penalty based on your program's requirements.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Program account: RP (Payroll)

RP account includes subsidiary ledger accounts for revenue type 60. Certain types of accounts, such as religious organizations, estates for the deceased, registered charity, non-profit organizations and others, may be identified as penalty exempt. The system does not assess any penalties on these accounts. However, penalty exempt accounts remain subject to interest charges.

Penalty - Late remitting

Late remittances (where deductions were remitted voluntarily but late) are subject to a penalty of:

  • 3% if the amount is one to 3 days late
  • 5% if it is 4 or 5 days late
  • 7% if it is 6 or 7 days late
  • 10% if it is more than 7 days late or if no amount is remitted

No penalty is charged if less than $10. The 3% rate for 3 days late or less includes a one business day grace period (system tolerance). The grace day is always the next business day following the remittance due date.

Action

No calculation is required to apply the penalty. A late remitting penalty (LRP) is assessed by the system on payments posted to payroll deductions accounts where the following conditions are met:

  • The payment is $833.34 or more if it is late 3 days or less
  • The payment is $700 or more if it is 4 to 5 days
  • The payment is $642.86 or more if it is 6 to 7 days
  • The payment is $600 or more if is more than 7 days late
  • The payment is coded late
  • The account is not penalty exempt
  • The account is not coded bankrupt

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Subsection 227(9) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to remit

An employer is liable for a penalty for failure to remit or pay an amount deducted or withheld as required. Unless the failure is made knowingly or under circumstances amounting to gross negligence, the penalty equals 10% of the amount that is not remitted or paid.

Action

The system calculates and charges interest from the effective interest date (EID) stated on the PAYDAC Assessment transcript. For Last Year (LY) PIER and PD4R assessments, the EID stated on the Assessment transcript (PD17) should be January 15, of the year following the year being assessed.

Proactive relief may apply for interest charges.

Subsection 227(8) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to file electronically

Every filer (other than a registered charity) who annually submits more than 50 information returns (slips) of one information return type, must file electronically. Filers who fail to do so are subject to a mandatory electronic filing penalty. The method for calculating the penalty will depend on the number of slips filed:

  • $1,500 for 501 - 2,500 information slips
