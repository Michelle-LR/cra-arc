CERB

No penalty or interest is payable on any CERB amount owing to Her Majesty in right of Canada as a result of an erroneous payment or overpayment.

Penalty - Instalments

Every person who fails to pay tax instalments by the due date is liable to a penalty equal to 50% of the amount by which the interest payable by the person under section 161 in respect of all instalments for the year exceeds the greater of:

  • $1,000
  • 25% of the interest that would have been payable by the person under section 161 in respect of all instalments for the year if no instalment had been made for that year

Action

No calculation is required to apply the penalty. The penalty is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Act

Section  163.1 of the Income Tax Act

Interest - Instalments

In addition to the interest payable under subsection 161(1), where a taxpayer who is required to pay income tax instalments has failed to do so by the due date, the taxpayer shall pay to the Receiver General interest at the prescribed rate on the amount that the taxpayer failed to pay computed from the day on or before which the amount was required to be paid to:

  • The day of payment
  • The beginning of the period in respect of which the taxpayer is required to pay interest under subsection 161(1), whichever is earlier

Action

No calculation is required to apply the interest. The interest is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for interest charges.

Act

Subsection 161(2) of the Income Tax Act

Interest - Late payments

Where, at any time after a taxpayer's balance-due date for a taxation year, the total payable exceeds the amount paid (including an amount applied by the Minister), the taxpayer shall pay interest at the prescribed rate on the amount, computed for the period during which that excess is outstanding.

Action

No calculation is required to apply the interest. The interest is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for interest charges.

Act

Subsection 161(1) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to file by trustee

Every trustee who fails to file a return when and as required is liable to a penalty of $10 for each day, not exceeding $50. This includes trustee in bankruptcy, assignee, liquidator, curator, receiver, trustee or committee and every agent or other person administering, managing, winding up, controlling or otherwise dealing with the property, business, estate or income of a person.

Action

No calculation is required to apply the penalty. The penalty is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Act

Subsection 162(3) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Late filing/Failure to file

Every person who fails to file a return for a taxation year as and when required is liable to pay a penalty equal to the sum of:

  • 5% of the tax payable for the taxation year that was not paid by the day the return was required to be filed
  • The amount obtained when 1% of the amount in the first bullet is multiplied by the number of complete months, not exceeding 12, from the day the return was required to the day the return is filed

Action

No calculation is required to apply the penalty. The penalty is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Act

Subsection  162(1) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to file pursuant to a demand to file

Every person who fails to file a return when required pursuant to a demand issued under subsection 150(2) after a failure to file penalty was payable for any of the 3 preceding taxation years is liable to a penalty equal to:

  • 10% of the tax payable for the taxation year that was not paid by the day the return was required to be filed
  • The amount obtained when 2% of the amount in the first bullet is multiplied by the number of complete months, not exceeding 20, from the day the return was required to the day the return is filed

Action

You need to calculate the amount of penalty based on your program's requirements.

BCD programs do not apply proactive relief for this type of penalty.

Also, to request the relief, the taxpayer can:

  • File the paper form RC4288 Request for Taxpayer Relief
  • Submit their requests electronically via E-RC4288 through MyBA or RAC

Act

Subsection 162(2) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to file in appropriate manner - tax preparer

Every tax preparer who fails to file a return of income for a taxation year as required by subsection 150.1(2.3) is liable to a penalty equal to $25 for each failure in respect of a return of an individual.

Action

No calculation is required to apply the penalty. The penalty is calculated automatically by the system.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Act

Subsection 162(7.3) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - Failure to report an amount

Every person is liable to a penalty who:

  • Fails to report an amount, equal to or greater than $500, required to be included in computing the person's income in a return for a taxation year
  • Had failed to report an amount, equal to or greater than $500, required to be included in computing the person's income in a return filed for any of the three preceding taxation years
  • Is not liable to a penalty under subsection 163(2) in respect of the unreported amount

The amount of the penalty is equal to the lesser of:

  • 10% of the unreported amount
  • The amount determined by the formula

0.5 x (A – B)

where
A is the total of the amounts that would be determined under paragraphs 163(2)(a) to (g) if subsection (2) applied in respect of the unreported amount, and
B is any amount deducted or withheld under subsection 153(1) that may reasonably be considered to be in respect of the unreported amount.

Action

You need to calculate the amount of penalty based on your program's requirements.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Penalty - Failure to provide information on a form

Every person who fails to provide any information on a prescribed form when and as required is liable to a penalty of $100 for every failure unless, in the case of:

  • Information required in respect of another person, a reasonable effort was made by the person to obtain the information
  • A failure to provide a SIN on a return of income, the person had applied for the assignment of the number and had not received it by the time the return was filed

Action

You need to calculate the amount of penalty based on your program's requirements.

Proactive relief may apply for this type of penalty.

Subsection 162(5) of the Income Tax Act

Penalty - False statements or omissions (gross negligence)

The deliberate and knowing submission of fictitious information, when proven, will be met with severe consequences.

Every person who knowingly (or under circumstances amounting to gross negligence), makes a false statement or omission in a return is liable to a penalty of the greater of:

  • $100
  • 50% of the total of:
    • If the false statement or omission is relevant to the determination of an amount payable, the amount that the amount payable exceeds the amount that would be payable if the amount payable were determined based on of the information provided in the return
    • If the false statement or omission is relevant to the determination of a refund or any other payment, the amount that would be the refund (or other payment) payable if it were determined on the basis of the information provided in the return exceeds the amount of the refund (or other payment) payable to the person

Action

You need to calculate the amount of penalty based on your program's requirements.

BCD programs do not apply proactive relief for this type of penalty.

Also, to request the relief, the taxpayer can:

  • File the paper form RC4288 Request for Taxpayer Relief
  • Submit their requests electronically via E-RC4288 through MyBA or RAC

Meaning of a remote store

Generally, a remote store refers to a store that during the previous 12-month period, made more than 50% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities and is in one of the following situations:

  • The store is located more than 350 kilometres from the nearest community with a population of 5,000 or more
  • Surface transportation is not available year-round on paved or gravelled roads linking the store to the nearest community
  • The store is within 10 kilometres of a reserve

A store in a remote location but that doesn't meet the sales condition because it does not have a sales history may still qualify as a remote store based on a reasonable expectation of such sales. An example of a store without a sales history is a newly opened store.

Vendors who choose to operate as a remote store must keep records of their sales made to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities.
