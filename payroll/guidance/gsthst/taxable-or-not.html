  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Accupuncturist
    Important details and requirements

    Requirements

    The acupuncture services must be rendered by a person who has one of the following:

    • Licensed or certified in the province to practise acupuncture
    • Equivalent qualifications in a province where acupuncture is not regulated
    • Licensed or certified to provide services exempted by another provision (for example, doctors, nurses, naturopathic doctors)

    Learn more: B-110 Application of the GST/HST to the Practise of Acupuncture

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, paragraph 7(l)

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Audiologist
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Audiologists diagnose and manage hearing loss and other hearing issues.

    Audiologist must have a Master's degree to practise in their profession and the profession is regulated in every province or territory.

    Requirements

    The services must be rendered to the patient that the treatment is being paid for.

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, paragraph 7(g)

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Chiropractor
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Chiropractic is a pseudoscientific alternative medicine that proposes adjustments to the spine can improve nerve function.

    Chiropractic is regulated across Canada. Provincial colleges establish education requirements to practise in the province or territory.

    Requirements

    The chiropractic services must be rendered by a person who is certified or licensed to practise chiropractic in the province or territory.

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, paragraph 7(b)

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Midwife
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Midwifery is regulated in all provinces and territories, except Prince Edward Island and Yukon (both have their applications for regulation pending).

    Midwife must be certified or licensed to practise midwifery in a regulated province or territory.

    Requirements

    The midwifery services must be rendered by a person who is certified or licensed to practise midwifery in a regulated province or has equivalent qualifications in a province where midwifery is not regulated.

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, paragraph 7(k)

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Naturopathic doctor
    Important details and requirements

    Requirements

    Service by a naturopathic doctor qualify for exemption if:

    • The supply is made after February 11, 2014
    • The practitioner has graduated from an accredited naturopathic medical program and holds a valid licence to practise as a naturopathic doctor
    • The supply is a naturopathic service rendered to an individual

    Learn more: B-109, Application of the GST/HST to the Practice of Naturopathic Doctors

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, paragraph 7(m)

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Nurse
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Nursing involves a combination of assessing the health care needs of a patient, determining the appropriate treatment or equipment, providing supportive, preventative, therapeutic, palliative and rehabilitative treatments and administering medications.

    Nurses must have a Master's degree to practise in their profession and the profession is regulated in every province or territory.

    Requirements

    The nursing services must be rendered by a nurse to a patient within a nurse-patient relationship, regardless of the location.

    A nursing service can be rendered in a hospital, a patient's home or any other location.

    Examples

    • Registered nurse
    • Registered nursing assistant
    • Registered practical nurse
    • Registered psychiatric nurse

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, section 6

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of practioners: Pharmacist
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    The services that pharmacists are approved to perform have been expanding.

    Pharmacist must have a Master's degree to practise in their profession and the profession is regulated in every province or territory.

    Requirements

    Services performed by a pharmacist, other than dispensing fees, are exempt when rendered in a pharmacist-patient relationship for the promotion of the health of the patient.

    Examples

    Prescribe some medications, make changes or substitutions to prescriptions, administer vaccines, order and interpret lab tests.

  • Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of health care service: Social work
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Social work includes services rendered to a caregiver of the individual (other than to another social worker).

    Requirements

    The social work services must be:

    • Rendered to an individual within a professional-client relationship for the prevention, assessment or remediation of a physical, emotional, behavioural, or mental disorder or disability
    • Rendered by a person that is licensed or certified to practise the profession of social work or, in Yukon and Nunavut, has equivalent qualifications

    Learn more: Excise and GST/HST News - No. 103
