  • Tax status: Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of health care services: Provided by a nurse
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Nursing involves a combination of assessing the health care needs of a patient, determining the appropriate treatment or equipment, providing supportive, preventative, therapeutic, palliative and rehabilitative treatments and administering medications.

    Nurses must have a Master's degree to practise in their profession and the profession is regulated in every province or territory.

    Requirements

    The nursing services must be rendered by a nurse to a patient within a nurse-patient relationship, regardless of the location.

    A nursing service can be rendered in a hospital, a patient's home or any other location.

    Examples

    • Registered nurse
    • Registered nursing assistant
    • Registered practical nurse
    • Registered psychiatric nurse

  • Tax status: Exempt

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of health care services: Provided by a physician or a dentist - Consultations, diagnoses, treatments, referrals, surgeries, administering vaccinations and requesting bloodwork and other diagnostic services
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    The purpose of a qualifying health care supply must be for one of the following:

    • Maintaining health
    • Preventing disease
    • Treating, relieving or remediating an injury, illness, disorder or disability
    • Assisting (other than financially) an individual in coping with an injury, illness, disorder or disability
    • Providing palliative health care

    Requirements

    To qualify, the supply:

    • Must be a consultative, diagnostic, treatment or other health care service rendered to an individual
    • Cannot be for cosmetic purposes, unless it is covered by a provincial health plan

    ETA: Schedule V, Part II, section 5
