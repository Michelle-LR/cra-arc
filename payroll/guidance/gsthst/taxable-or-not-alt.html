    Type of supply: Sale or lease - Commercial real property

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situations

    The sale or lease of commercial real property is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% in most common situations.

    A leasehold interest or the assignment of a commercial lease is considered a sale of commercial real property and is taxable at the applicable rate. The sale of the property is also taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when sold by an individual or personal trust where:

    • Immediately before the time ownership or possession of the property is transferred, it is capital property that was used primarily (more than 50%) in a business carried on by the individual or personal trust with a reasonable expectation of profit.
    • The sale is made in the course of a business of the individual or personal trust
    • The sale is made in the course of an adventure or concern in the nature of trade and the individual or personal trust has elected to have the sale treated as a taxable supply.

    Exempt situations

    In certain situations the sale of real property made by an individual or a personal trust may be GST/HST exempt. For more information our GST/HST Rulings provides free technical rulings and interpretations. You may contact then at 1-800-959-8287.

    Example

    A building owner leases the building to a person and under the terms of the lease the person is entitled to assign the leasehold interest to a third party for consideration. Such an assignment is considered to be a sale of real property since a lessee that transfers its leasehold interest to a third party is transferring the ownership of the real property (in other words, the ownership of the leasehold interest) and therefore is making a sale of real property. The assignor in such a case is subject to the same provisions in respect of GST/HST obligations and entitlements that govern any other sale of real property.

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale - Previously-owned residential housing

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    The sale of used residential housing is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when any of the following applies:

    • It is renovated (substantially or not) by the owner who is also a builder
    • It was substantially renovated before the sale of it or for the purpose of selling it
    • It was purchased, renovated and resold in a business venture
    • The vendor claimed Input Tax Credits (ITCs) on the acquisition or any "improvement" to the home
    • The residence was used as short-term accommodation
    • The property was used in a business or commercial activity

    Substantial renovation is generally when all of the interior of the existing building has been virtually removed or replaced (in other words, gutted) and, upon completion, the renovated or altered building is, or forms part of, a residential complex.

    The sale of used residential housing is exempt when:

    • It is sold by someone who occupied the home as their primary residence,
    • The home has been occupied by the owner (who is not a builder) and renovations are not considered to be "substantial"
    • A builder sells a unit in a condominium complex that was converted by the builder from use as a multiple unit residential complex to use as a condominium complex
    • The home has been repossessed by the builder (i.e., acquired by way of exempt sale)

    Schedule V, Part I, s.2 exempts the sale by the owner who is not a builder.

    Example

    Tax status if all requirements are met:

    ETA: 123(1), 190(1) and schedule V, Part I, s.4 and s.5

  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale or lease - Vacant land that is not a farmland

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situation

    The lease of vacant land is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% on the portion that is not for a share of zero-rated crops. A sharecrop agreement is when one farmer rents farmland to another for a percentage of the land's farm production.

    The sale of vacant land is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when either:

    • It is sold by an individual or personal trust and the land, at any time during the ownership, was severed into more than two parts, or
    • The land is sold by either a partnership or a corporation.

    Zero-rated situation

    The lease of land is zero-rated to the extent that the consideration for the lease of the land is a share of the farmland's production of zero-rated crops. It is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% for any lease payment that is not for a share of zero-rated crops.

    Exempt situation

    The sale of the vacant land is exempt when sold by an individual or personal trust and

    • It is sold to a relative for their personal use and enjoyment
    • the land was not severed into parts while the individual or personal trust held title of the property
    • Sales of farmland to related persons

    Paragraph 9(2)(c) of Part I of Schedule V does not exempt the sale of vacant land in ceratin situation

    Example

    Mr. Acres owns a three-hectare parcel of land. In 2012, he subdivided that parcel into one two-hectare lot and one one-hectare lot then sold the one-hectare lot. That sale is exempt. In January 2020, Mr. Acres subdivided the remaining two-hectare lot into two lots of one hectare each and sold those lots to people who are not related to him. Until that sale, the vacant land had been used primarily for Mr. Acres' personal use and enjoyment. Since these one-hectare lots were produced by the further severance of the two-hectare lot which had itself been severed from the original three-hectare parcel, his sale of these one-hectare lots to people who are not related to him is GST/HST taxable.

    Tax status: Taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% depending on the location

    ETA: 136(2) and schedule V, I 9(2)©
