  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale or lease - Commercial real property

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situations

    The sale or lease of commercial real property is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% in most common situations.

    A leasehold interest or the assignment of a commercial lease is considered a sale of commercial real property and is taxable at the applicable rate.

    The sale of the property is also taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when sold by an individual or personal trust where:

    • Immediately before the time ownership or possession of the property is transferred, it is capital property that was used primarily (more than 50%) in a business carried on by the individual or personal trust with a reasonable expectation of profit.
    • The sale is made in the course of a business of the individual or personal trust
    • The sale is made in the course of an adventure or concern in the nature of trade and the individual or personal trust has elected to have the sale treated as a taxable supply.

    Exempt situations

    In certain situations the sale of real property made by an individual or a personal trust may be GST/HST exempt. For more information our GST/HST Rulings provides free technical rulings and interpretations. You may contact then at 1-800-959-8287.

    Learn more: Tax status if all requirements are met: Taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% depending on the location

    ETA: Subsections 1236(1) and 221(2)

    Taxable situation

    Exempt situationShort-term accommodation (such as in a rooming or boarding house) is exempt where the charge is not more than $20 per day. Taxable situationThe supply is taxable if the accommodation is provided by a registrant and the rent payable is more than $20 for each day of occupancy or if it is a commercial rental or lease (includes campground rentals, trailer park, most hotel accommodations).

    Example

    A “bed and breakfast” establishment charges $20 per day for its rooms and separately charges $5 per day for breakfast if breakfast is requested. Rooms are rented on a short-term basis. In this example, there are two supplies:

  • the supply of the room is exempt (whether or not the establishment is a residential complex) while the supply of the breakfast is taxable.

    If the establishment did not charge separately for the breakfast and charged a total of $25 per day for a room and breakfast, the full charge would be taxable because the total cost is more than $20 per day.

    If the individual renting a room does not request the breakfast and pays $20 per day for the room, they would not pay GST/HST for the room.

    • Tax status if all requirements are met: Exempt

  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Lease - Residential parking space

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situations

    The lease of a parking space is taxable when an individual is not using the parking space as incidential to for the use and enjoyment of the residential complex.

    Exempt situations

    The lease of a parking space throughout a period of at least 1 month to a lessee of a residential unit is exempt if one of the following apply:

    • The parking space forms part of the residential complex or trailer park
    • The parking space is supplied by the owner as incidental to the use and enjoyment of the complex, unit or site of the unit as a place of residence.

      This exemption also applies to the lease of a parking space supplied with a floating home when the lease is for a period of at least 1 month at a mooring facility and the parking space is incidental to the use and enjoyment of the floating home as a place of residence for individuals.

    Learn more: GST/HST Memorandum 19-2-2 Residential Real Property - Rentals

    Example

    Example 1: A landlord and tenant sign a one year lease of a condominium unit that includes a parking spot in the building. The lease of the parking space is exempt because the parking space forms part of the residential complex as incidental to the use and enjoyment of the condominium unit as a place of residence.

    Example 2: A homeowner advertises a parking space available in their driveway to employees of a nearby worksite. Since the worksite does not have enough parking spaces for all employees, one of the employees rents the private parking space from the owner of the house. In that situation the parking is taxable at 5%. 13 or 15% since the parking is not incidental to the use of the home by the person using the parking space. However if the owner is not a GST/HST registrant, then they do not collect the GST/HST from the individual renting the parking space.

    Tax status if all requirements are met: Exempt

    Taxable situation

    For mixed-use real property, the commercial portion being sold is deemed to be a separate supply than the residential portion. For the tax status of each portion, if it would have been exempt when sold on its own, that portion will be exempt when sold together.

    Taxable situation

    The commercial portion is taxable in the most common situations. This includes property sold by an individual or personal trust where:

    • immediately before the time ownership or possession of the property is transferred, the property is capital property used primarily (more than 50%) in a business carried on by the individual or personal trust with a reasonable expectation of profit
    • the sale is made in the course of a business of the individual or personal trust
    • the sale is made in the course of an adventure or concern in the nature of trade where the individual or personal trust has elected to have the sale treated as a taxable supply.

    Exempt situations

    The residential portion is usually exempt. There are also certain situations where the sale of the commercial portion would be GST/HST exempt.

    Learn more: GST.GST Memorandum 19-4-1 Commercial Real Property - Sales and Rentals - Canada.ca"

    Note: The recipient of a taxable sale of real property is required to remit the GST/HST directly to the Receiver General for Canada when:

    • the supplier is a non-resident,
    • non-residential real property is sold to a GST/HST registrant, or
    • residential real property is sold to a GST/HST registrant that is not an individual

        Example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situations

    The lease of residential housing is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when one of the following apply:

  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale - Previously-occupied residential housing

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situations

    The sale of previously-occupied housing is taxable:

    • It is renovated by the owner who is also a builder
    • It was substantially renovated before the sale of it or for the purpose of selling it
    • It was purchased, renovated and resold in a business venture
    • The vendor claimed Input Tax Credits (ITCs) on the acquisition or any "improvement" to the home
    • The residence was used as short-term accommodation
    • The property was used in a business or commercial activity

    Exempt situations

    The sale of previously-occupied residential housing is exempt when:

    • It is sold by someone who occupied the home as their primary residence,
    • The home has been occupied by the owner (who is not a builder) and renovations are not considered to be "substantial"
    • A builder sells a unit in a condominium complex that was converted by the builder from use as a multiple unit residential complex to use as a condominium complex
    • The home has been repossessed by the builder (i.e., acquired by way of exempt sale)

      Substantial renovation is generally when all of the interior of the existing building has been virtually removed or replaced (in other words, gutted) and, upon completion, the renovated or altered building is, or forms part of, a residential complex.

      If you operated a bed and breakfast, see https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/forms-publications/publications/gi-007/operating-a-bed-breakfast-your-home.html">GST/HST Info Sheet GI-007

      Example

      An individual purchased a unit in a condominium building. After residing in the condominium unit, the individual sold it and purchased a larger home. The sale of the condominium unit is exempt because it was the individual's residence.

    Tax status if all requirements are met: Exempt

    ETA: Subsections 123(1) and 190(1), Schedule V, Part I, sections 2, 4 and 5

  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale or lease - Vacant land that is not a farmland

    More information and example

    Taxable situation

    Taxable situation

    The lease of vacant land is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% on the portion that is not for a share of zero-rated crops.

    A sharecrop agreement is when one farmer rents farmland to another for a percentage of the land's farm production.

    The sale of vacant land is taxable when either:

    • It is sold by an individual or personal trust and the land, at any time during the ownership, was severed into more than two parts, or
    • The land is sold by either a partnership or a corporation.

    Zero-rated situation

    The lease of land is zero-rated to the extent that the consideration for the lease of the land is a share of the farmland's production of zero-rated crops. It is otherwise taxable for any lease payment that is not for a share of zero-rated crops.

    Exempt situation

    The sale of the vacant land is exempt when sold by an individual or personal trust and

    • It is sold to a relative for their personal use and enjoyment
    • the land was not severed into parts while the individual or personal trust held title of the property
    • Sales of farmland to related persons

    Paragraph 9(2)(c) of Part I of Schedule V does not exempt the sale of vacant land in ceratin situation

    If land is not part of a residential complex, as that term is defined in subsection 123(1) of the Act, immediately prior to the sale of the land, it does not qualify for exemption under those sections of the Act that would otherwise apply to a sale of a residential complex or in interest in one. This may occur, for example, where land has been severed from a residential complex and supplied separately from the land upon which the complex is situated. Consequently, a sale of such land is not exempt under section 2, 3, 4 or 5 of Part I of Schedule V, which exempt, under certain conditions, a sale of a residential complex or an interest in one. The sale of the land would not usually qualify for exemption under section 5.2 of Part I of Schedule V, which exempts the sale of land that forms part of a residential complex. Section 9 of Part I of Schedule V may apply to exempt the sale of land by an individual provided that the sale meets the other requirements of the section.

    Example

    Mr. Acres owns a three-hectare parcel of land. In 2012, he subdivided that parcel into one two-hectare lot and one one-hectare lot then sold the one-hectare lot. That sale is exempt.

    In January 2020, Mr. Acres subdivided the remaining two-hectare lot into two lots of one hectare each and sold those lots to people who are not related to him. Until that sale, the vacant land had been used primarily for Mr. Acres' personal use and enjoyment.

    Since these one-hectare lots were produced by the further severance of the two-hectare lot which had itself been severed from the original three-hectare parcel, his sale of these one-hectare lots to people who are not related to him is GST/HST taxable.

    Tax status: Taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% depending on the location

    Psychologists treat emotional and mental suffering. Unlike psychiatrists, they are not medical doctors and cannot prescribe medication.

    Psychologist must have a Master's degree to practise in their profession and the profession is regulated in every province or territory.

    Requirements

    The services must be rendered to the patient that the treatment is being paid for.

    Examples

    Prescribe some medications, make changes or substitutions to prescriptions, administer vaccines, order and interpret lab tests.

    • Type of supply: Health care services
    • Type of health care services: Provided by for social work
    Important details and requirements

    Details

    Social work includes services rendered to a caregiver of the individual (other than to another social worker).

    Requirements

    The social work services must be:

    • Rendered to an individual within a professional-client relationship for the prevention, assessment or remediation of a physical, emotional, behavioural, or mental disorder or disability
    • Rendered by a person that is licensed or certified to practise the profession of social work or, in Yukon and Nunavut, has equivalent qualifications
