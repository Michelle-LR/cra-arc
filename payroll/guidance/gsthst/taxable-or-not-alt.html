Find out if a supply is taxable, zero-rated or exempt

The interactive tax tool (ITT) gives you personalized results based on your answers to a series of questions. The tool uses plain language to explain the most common tax situations. It is provided as a guideline only and does not replace the law.

The accuracy of your results depends on the accuracy of the information you enter. Later calculations made by the CRA may be different.

This tool does not collect or store the information you enter.

Whether or not to charge the GST/HST may depend on who the supply is made to and who makes the supply.

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Residential - Sale of previously-owned residential housing

    Explanation

    The sale of used residential housing is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when any of the following applies:

    • It is renovated (substantially or not) by the owner who is also a builder
    • It was substantially renovated before the sale of it or for the purpose of selling it
    • It was purchased, renovated and resold in a business venture
    • The vendor claimed Input Tax Credits (ITCs) on the acquisition or any "improvement" to the home
    • The residence was used as short-term accommodation
    • The property was used in a business or commercial activity

    Substantial renovation is generally when all of the interior of the existing building has been virtually removed or replaced (in other words, gutted) and, upon completion, the renovated or altered building is, or forms part of, a residential complex.

    The sale of used residential housing is exempt when:

    • It is sold by someone who occupied the home as their primary residence,
    • The home has been occupied by the owner (who is not a builder) and renovations are not considered to be "substantial"
    • A builder sells a unit in a condominium complex that was converted by the builder from use as a multiple unit residential complex to use as a condominium complex
    • The home has been repossessed by the builder (i.e., acquired by way of exempt sale)

    Tax status if all requirements are met:

    • ETA: Schedule V, Part I, s.2 exempts the sale by the owner who is not a builder
    • ETA: Subsection 123(1) defines "builder"
    • ETA: Subsection 190(1) deems a person that has substantially renovated a house to be a builder (in the most common circumstances)
    • ETA: Schedule V, Part I, s.4 and s.5 exempts the sale of a used residential property unless the builder substantially renovates or hires someone to substantially renovate the home

  • Business activity: Most businesses

    Category of supply: Real property

    Type of supply: Sale or lease of vacant land (except farmland)

    Explanation

    Taxable situation

    The lease of vacant land is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% on the portion that is not for a share of zero-rated crops. A sharecrop agreement is when one farmer rents farmland to another for a percentage of the land's farm production.

    The sale of vacant land is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% when either:

    • It is sold by an individual or personal trust and the land, at any time during the ownership, was severed into more than two parts, or
    • The land is sold by either a partnership or a corporation.

    Zero-rated situation

    The lease of land is zero-rated to the extent that the consideration for the lease of the land is a share of the farmland's production of zero-rated crops. It is taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% for any lease payment that is not for a share of zero-rated crops.

    Exempt situation

    The sale of the vacant land is exempt when sold by an individual or personal trust and

    • It is sold to a relative for their personal use and enjoyment
    • the land was not severed into parts while the individual or personal trust held title of the property
    • Sales of farmland to related persons

    Tax status: Taxable at 5%, 13% or 15% depending on the location

    • ETA: Paragraph 9(2)(c) of Part I of Schedule V does not exempt the sale of vacant land in ceratin situation
    • ETA: Ss 136(2) Combined supply of real property
    • ETA: Sch V, Part I. para. 9(2)(c) discusses the severing or division of land
