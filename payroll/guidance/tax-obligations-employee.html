Employee and employer relationship: Understand the tax obligations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language, including additional examples and legislative references.

Depending if the worker is an employee, self-employed or an incorporated employee the tax obligations may be different.

If you are not sure if the worker is an employee, continue to: Determine if the worker is an employee or self-employed

Understanding the tax obligations based on the individual:

  • Employee
  • Employer
Employee

Understanding the employee's tax obligations:

  • Receiving the slip
  • Reporting the income earned on the employee's income tax return

Receiving the slip

Generally, the employer must provide the employee with a T4 slip which includes any payment made regardless if the income is taxable or not.

Learn more about the options if the employee does not expect to get the slip: Get a copy of a slip

Reporting the income earned on the employee's income tax return

When filing the T1 return, the employee uses the amounts from the T4 slip.

Learn more: T4 slip: Statement of Remuneration Paid

Employer

Understanding the employer's tax obligations:

  • Withholding and remitting deductions
  • Reporting and distributing the slip
  • Reporting on the employer's income tax return

Withholding and remitting deductions

If the employer makes a payment related to employment income, the withholding and remitting are different depending on who the payment is made to and the type of payment.

Learn more: Get ready to make deductions

Reporting and distributing the slip

Generally, if the employer makes a payment in an employment relationship, the employer must fill out a T4 slip.

If the employer distributes the slips late or does not distribute, the employer may be assessed a penalty of $25 per day per slip with a minimum penalty of $100 and a maximum of $2,500.

Learn more:

  • T4 slip - Information for employers
  • Obligations to distribute the slip: Distribute the slips

Reporting the payment on the employer's income tax return

Generally, the employer includes the payment made to the employee in their calculation of business income and expenses using one of the following form when filing their income tax return:

  • T1 return

    • Form T2125, Statement of Business or Professional Activities
    • Form T2042, Statement of Farming Activities
    • Form T2121, Statement of Fishing Activities

    Learn more: Business or professional income

  • T2 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: Completing your corporation income tax return
