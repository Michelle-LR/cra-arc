Employer

Understanding the employer's tax obligations:

  • Payroll obligations
  • Income tax obligations

Payroll obligations

Generally, any person must open a payroll account (RP), calculate payroll deductions and contributions, remit (pay) deductions and contributions, and file information returns if the payment is:

  • Pay salaries, wages (including advances), bonuses, vacation pay, or tips to employees
  • Provide certain taxable benefits to employees (for example, an automobile or allowances)

Generally, the employer must withhold and remit the income tax, EI, CPP or GST/HST on payments made related to employment income.

If the employer makes a payment in an employment relationship, the employer must fill out a T4 slip.

Learn more: Payroll

Income tax obligations

The employer must report and pay tax on all income earned from taxable activities. The income is the net earnings from employment, property, investments and benefits. To report and pay tax, the employer must calculate the business income and expenses using one of the following form when filing their income tax return:

  • T1 return
    • Form T2125, Statement of Business or Professional Activities
    • Form T2042, Statement of Farming Activities
    • Form T2121, Statement of Fishing Activities

    Learn more: Business or professional income

  • T2 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: Completing your corporation income tax return

  • T5013 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information

    Learn more: T5013 Partnership Information Return filing requirements

GST/HST obligations

Generally, any person must open a GST/HST account (RT), collect, file and remit (pay) the GST/HST if they provide goods or services and earn more than $30,000 over 4 calendar quarters.

Learn more: Collect, file, and remit (pay) the GST/HST

Other tax obligations

Depending on the type of industry, the employer may have other tax obligations, for example:

  • Import-export account (RM), for businesses that imports goods or sells goods or services abroad
  • Information returns account (RZ), for businesses that files information returns, such as tax-free savings account (TFSA), T5, T5013, and more
  • Registered charity account (RR), for businesses that are a charity organization
  • Underused housing tax account (RU), for corporations that files a Underused Housing Tax return for each of their properties in Canada.
  • Fuel charge account (CT), for businesses that need to include a fuel charge on products
  • Excise duties account (RD), for businesses that need to charge excise duty on products
  • Excise tax and special levies account (RE), for businesses that need to charge excise tax on products
  • Air travelers security charge account (RG), for air carriers that charge the air travellers security charge
  • Insurance premium tax account (RN), for brokers and agents who place contracts of insurance
  • Luxury tax account (LT), for businesses that need to charge luxury tax on the sale or importation of select luxury items
Employee

Income tax obligations

Employees who are residents of Canada are required to report their worldwide income from all sources.

If the employee is a non-resident, they are subject to Canadian income tax on most Canadian-sourced income paid or credited to them during the year unless all or part of that income is exempt under a tax treaty.

When filing the T1 return, the employee uses the amounts from the T4 slip.

Receiving the slip

Generally, the employer must provide the employee with a T4 slip which includes any payment made regardless if the income is taxable or not.
