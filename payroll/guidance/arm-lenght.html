  • Title: AFB Janitorial Services Inc. v. MNR

    • Result: Self-employed worker
    • Court: Tax Court of Canada
    • Industry: Administrative and support, waste management and remediation services
    Summary
    • Act: Canada Pension Plan
    • Reference: 2023 TCC 94
    • File number(s): 2021-126(CPP), 2021-143(EI)

    Summary of court case

    Intent (factor 1)
    Self-employed worker
    Control (factor 2)
    • Worker has freedom to determine how work should be done
    • Worker does not have the right to refuse work from the payer
    • Worker sets their own hours and schedule
    • Worker is not monitored or supervised by the payer
    • Worker works at the payer's workplace
    • Payer determines what work is to be done
    • Payer imposes a dress code or requires a uniform
    • Payer does not provide the worker with training
    Ownership of tools (factor 3)
    Payer owns the most important tools or equipment
    Subcontracting of work (factor 4)
    Worker can delegate or subcontract work without notifying the payer
    Risk (factor 5)
    Worker is personally at risk of loss
    Reimbursement (factor 5)
    Worker is not reimbursed for work-related travel expenses

  • Title: Payette c. ministre du revenu nationale

    • Result: Self-employed worker
    • Court: Tax Court of Canada
    • Industry: Finance and insurance
    Summary
    • Act: Employment Insurance Act
    • Reference: 2023 TCC 9
    • File number(s): 2018-1773(EI)

    Summary of court case

    Intent (factor 1)
    Self-employed worker
    Control (factor 2)
    • Worker does not have freedom to determine how work should be done
    • Worker can accept or refuse work from the payer
    • Worker sets their own hours and schedule
    • Worker is monitored or supervised by the payer
    • Worker works in mobile locations
    • Worker determines what work is to be done
    • Payer does not impose a dress code or does not require a uniform
    • Payer does not provide the worker with training
    Ownership of tools (factor 3)
    Both the worker and the payer owns the most important tools or equipment
    Subcontracting of work (factor 4)
    Worker can delegate or subcontract work without notifying the payer
    Risk (factor 5)
    Worker is personally at risk of loss
    Reimbursement (factor 5)
    Worker is not reimbursed for work-related travel expenses
