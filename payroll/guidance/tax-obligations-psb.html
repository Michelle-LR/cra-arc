Incorporated employee and payer relationship: Understand your tax obligations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language, including additional examples and legislative references.

Depending if the worker is an employee, an incorporated employee or is self-employed the tax obligations may be different.

Incorporated employees and payers have different tax obligations if the incorporated employees perform the services on behalf of their own corporation. An incorporated employee in this situation is considered a personal services business (PSB).

If you are not sure if the worker is an incorporated employee, continue to: Determine if the worker is an incorporated employee

Income tax obligations

Generally, the PSB must:

  • File T2 returns - Not eligible to claim the same tax deductions and expenses
  • Remit (pay) the income tax - Same as regular corporations

Learn more: Corporations

Payroll obligations

The PSB must withhold and remit deductions for any employees of the PSB's corporation (same as regular corporations). They must also report on a slip any payment made to their employees.

Continue to: Employee/Employer

CPP/QPP, EI and QPIP - PSB's contributions obligations

Generally, the PSB worker calculates their contributions and reports the following amounts when they fill out their T2 corporation income tax return:

  • EI - Only if the PSB worker participates in the special EI program
  • CPP - Both employer and employee portion
  • QPIP - Only if the PSB worker works in the Province of Quebec

GST/HST obligations

For the PSB, the GST/HST obligations are the same as other corporations.

Learn more: GST/HST for businesses

Example of non-compliance

Examples of non-compliance situation for PSB corporations:

  • Not reporting non-active business income on line 207 of the T2 returns and did not complete Schedule 7
  • Claiming the small business deduction on line 430 of the T2 return
  • Claiming other deductions that was not allowed on the T2 return
  • Claiming salary expense on the T2 return
  • Not filing T2 returns
  • Not registering for the GST/HST or filed the required returns

Reporting the payment on the income tax return

Generally, the payer includes the payment made to the worker in their calculation of business expenses using one of the following:

  • Form T2125 when filing your Income Tax and Benefit Return
  • Schedule 125, Income Statement Information when filing your Corporation Income Tax Return

Tax deductions, tax rates and eligible expenses for a PSB

Tax deductions and tax rates

A PSB is not eligible for:

  • General tax rate reduction
  • Small business deduction

This means that a PSB is subject to the full federal and provincial corporate tax rates on all taxable income, plus an additional 5%.

Eligible expenses

A PSB is limited to all of the following deduction expenses:

  • Salary and wages the corporation pays to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Any benefit or allowance the corporation provides to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Certain expenses of the corporation associated with selling property or negotiating contracts
  • Legal expenses incurred by the corporation in collecting amounts owing
