Incorporated employee and payer relationship: Understand your tax obligations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language, including additional examples and legislative references.

Depending if the worker is an employee, self-employed or an incorporated employee the tax obligations may be different.

Incorporated employees and payers have different tax obligations if the incorporated employees perform the services on behalf of their own corporation. An incorporated employee in this situation is considered a personal services business (PSB).

If you are not sure if the worker is an incorporated employee, continue to: Determine if the worker is an incorporated employee

Understanding the tax obligations based on the individual:

  • PSB
  • Payer - Payments made to a PSB
PSB

Understanding the PSB's tax obligations:

  • Reporting the income earned on the income tax return
  • Contributing to the CPP/QPP, EI and QPIP
  • Withholding, remitting and reporting deductions for employees of the PSB

Reporting the income earned on the income tax return

Generally, the payer must provide the PSB with a T4A slip which includes any fees or other amounts paid for services. Depending on the situation, the payer may provide the PSB with a T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 instead.

When filing the T2 return, the PSB must:

  • Only include the following eligible expenses on Schedule 125, Income Statement Information:
    • Salary and wages the corporation pays to their employees performing the services of a PSB
    • Any benefit or allowance the corporation provides to their employees performing the services of a PSB
    • Certain expenses of the corporation associated with selling property or negotiating contracts
    • Legal expenses incurred by the corporation in collecting amounts owing
  • Report on line 520 of Schedule 7, Aggregate Investment Income and Income Eligible for the Small Business Deduction the income from the PSB
  • Use the federal and provincial corporate tax rates on all taxable income, plus an additional 5% (the PSB is not eligible for the general tax rate reduction)
  • Not eligible to claim the small business deduction on line 430 of the T2 return

Contributing to the CPP/QPP, EI and QPIP

Generally, the PSB includes the amount of the payment received from the payer in their calculation of the following contributions when filing their income tax return:

  • EI - Only if the PSB participates in the special EI program
  • CPP - Both employer and employee portion
  • QPIP - Only if the PSB works in the Province of Quebec

Withholding, remitting and reporting deductions for employees of the PSB

The PSB must withhold, remit and report deductions for any employees of the PSB's corporation. This is the same as regular corporations. They must also report on a slip any payment made to their employees.

Payer

Understanding the payer's tax obligations:

  • Withholding, remitting and reporting deductions from the payment
  • Reporting the payment on the income tax return

Withholding, remitting and reporting deductions from the payment

Generally, if the payer pays a fee to a PSB to provide a service outside of an employment relationship or tenure of office, the payer must fill out a T4A slip. This includes payments of fees for services between businesses, including sole proprietors and corporations. Depending on the situation, the payer may need to fill out a T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 instead.

Learn more: Payments of fees for services

Reporting the payment on the income tax return

Generally, the payer includes the payment made to the worker in their calculation of business expenses using one of the following form when filing their income tax return:

  • T1 return

    Form T2125, Statement of Business or Professional Activities

    Learn more: Lines 13499 to 14300 – Self-employment income

  • T2 return

    Schedule 125, Income Statement Information
