Incorporated employee/Payer

Incorporated employees and payers have different tax obligations if the incorporated employees perform the services on behalf of their own corporation. Incorporated employees in this situation is considered a personal services business (PSB).

Understand the tax obligations depending on one of the following situation:

  • PSB workers

    Income tax obligations

    The tax deductions, tax rates and eligible expenses are different for a PSB.

    Generally, the PSB must:

    • File a T2 Corporation Income Tax Return - The PSB is not eligible to claim the same tax deductions and expenses
    • Remit (pay) the income tax - Same as regular Corporations
    • Fill out T4 slips for any employees of their corporation (same as regular corporations)

    Learn more: Add here

    CPP/QPP, EI and QPP obligations

    Generally, the self-employed worker calculates their contributions and reports the following amounts when they fill out their T1 income tax and benefit return:

    • EI - Only if the self-employed worker participates in the special EI program
    • CPP - Both employer and employee portion
    • QPIP - Only if the self-employed worker works in the Province of Quebec

    Learn more:

    • Contributions to the Canada Pension Plan
    • EI benefits for self-employed people
    • Québec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) | Revenu Québec

    GST/HST obligations

    For the PSB, the GST/HST obligations are the same as other corporations.

    Learn more: GST/HST for businesses

    Examples of non-compliance for PSB corporations

    Examples of non-compliance situation for PSB corporations:

    • Not reported non-active business income on line 207 of the T2 returns and they did not complete Schedule 7
    • Claimed the small business deduction on line 430 of the T2 return
    • Claimed other deductions that was not allowed on the T2 return
    • Claimed salary expense on the T2 return
    • Not filed T2 returns
    • Not registered for the GST/HST or filed the required returns
  • Payments made to incorporated employees (payer)

    Withholding and remitting payroll deductions

    Generally, the payer does not withhold or remit the following deductions on the payment made to the worker who is a PSB:

    • Income tax - Do not withhold or remit
    • EI - Do not withhold or remit
    • CPP - Do notwithhold or remit

    Learn more: Get ready to make deductions

    Remitting GST/HST

    Generally, the payer does not remit the following on the payment made to the worker who is a PSB:

    • GST/HST - Do not remit

    Learn more: GST/HST for businesses

    Reporting the payment on the PSB's slip

    Generally, the payer must report the payment made to the worker who is a PSB on the following slip:

    • T4A slip (or T4A-NR, T5018 or T1204 slip depending on the situation of the PSB)

    Learn more: Payments of fees for services.

    Reporting the payment on the payer's income tax return

    Generally, the payer must report the payment made to the worker who is a PSB on one of their following income tax return as a business expense:

    • T1 return - On line XXX or schedule XXX
    • T2 return - On line XXX or schedule XXX

Tax deductions, tax rates and eligible expenses for a PSB

A PSB is subject to the full federal and provincial corporate tax rates on all taxable income, plus an additional 5%.

A PSB is not eligible for:

  • General tax rate reduction
  • Small business deduction

Eligible expenses

A PSB is limited to all of the following deduction expenses:

  • Salary and wages the corporation pays to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Any benefit or allowance the corporation provides to its incorporated employee(s)
  • Certain expenses of the corporation associated with selling property or negotiating contracts
  • Legal expenses incurred by the corporation in collecting amounts owing
