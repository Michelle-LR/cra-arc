Understand the tax obligations

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language, including additional examples and legislative references.

Depending if the worker is an employee, self-employed or an incorporated employee the tax obligations may be different.

The incorporated employee and the payer have different tax obligations because the incorporated employee performs the services on behalf of their own corporation. An incorporated employee in this situation is considered a personal services business (PSB) for income tax purposes.

To understand the tax obligations, continue to one of the following depending on the type of relationship:

  • Employee and employer
  • Self-employed and payer
  • Incorporated employee and payer
