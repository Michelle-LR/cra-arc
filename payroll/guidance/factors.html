  1. Factors to consider to determine if the worker is an employee or self-employed

    You need to review the contract to determine in which province it was formed in because the multiple factors to consider are different.

    The factors to consider are based on where the contract was made, unless the contract says otherwise.

    What if the parties are not located in the same province

    When the parties involved in a contract are not in the same province or territory, multiple factors determine which jurisdiction's laws and regulations apply to the contract. The most common factors include the following:

    • The province or territory where the contract is considered to have been formed is significant (this is often the location where the final acceptance or agreement was communicated)
    • Where the parties have explicitly chosen the legal framework (the contract itself may specify which jurisdiction's laws should govern the agreement)
    • The provincial and territorial laws often have rules for determining which jurisdiction's laws apply in the absence of a specific choice of law in the contract
    • The jurisdiction where the offeror (the party making the initial offer) receives acceptance of the contract may be considered the jurisdiction where the contract was formed (this is if the parties negotiated the contract through methods like telephone, fax, or email)
    • The location of the parties involved may also play a role in determining which jurisdiction's laws apply (for example, if one party is in Quebec and another in Ottawa, the laws of Quebec might apply if that is where the contract was formed)
