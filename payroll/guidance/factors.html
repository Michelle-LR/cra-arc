Determine where the contract was formed

Content has been developed to provide guidance only. The guidance does not replace the law and does not replace a formal request for a ruling. No changes were made to the existing federal legislative requirement.

This guidance gives you the CRA's general view of a worker's employment status based on common situations. It can also be used to help you understand if changes to the terms of the contract or working arrangements changes a worker's employment status.

Type of information you need to use the guidance

To use the guidance, the following are examples of information you need to know:

  • Details of the contract
  • The worker's responsibilities
  • Who decides what work needs to be done
  • Who decides when, where and how the work is done
  • How the worker will be paid
  • If the engagement includes any corporate benefits or reimbursement for expenses

Determine where the contract was formed

The factors are different if the contract is formed in the province of Quebec or in any other province or territory (unless the contract states differently). If the contract is formed:

    • In a province other than Quebec or in a territory
    • Outside Canada and the employment is performed in a province other than Quebec, in a territory, or in another country

      • Continue to: Province or territory other than Quebec

    • In the province of Quebec
    • Outside Canada and the employment is performed in Quebec

      • Continue to: Province of Quebec
