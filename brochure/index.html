Get a social insurance number (SIN) or tax number

A SIN is a 9-digit number that is personal, confidential, and unique to you.

You need a SIN to:

  • receive benefits and credits
  • work in Canada
  • open most types of bank accounts

Go to Service Canada to apply for a SIN.

What to do if you can't get a SIN

If Service Canada is unable to give you a permanent or temporary SIN, the CRA may give you a temporary tax number (TTN) you may use to get benefits and credits, file taxes, and sign up for CRA's online services.

How to get a TTN

Complete your benefit or credit application or tax return without an account number. Attach a letter explaining why you do not have a SIN or can't get one.

To get a number faster, you may also include certified copies of documents that prove your identity, such as:

  • a passport
  • a driver's licence
  • a birth certificate or proof of birth
  • an immigration document showing that your spouse is sponsoring you (if your spouse has a SIN and is sponsoring you)
  • a letter from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada that says they are reviewing your file

Determine if you should file a tax return

In order to qualify for certain benefit payments, you will need to file a return, even if you have no tax to pay.

Full a full listing on when you must file, refer to: When you must file a tax return

Payments you may qualify for

Canada child benefit (CCB)

Provides monthly tax-free money. Eligible families can get up to $7,437 per year for each child under 6 and up to $6,275 per year, for each child aged 6 to 17.

Canada workers benefit (CWB)

Provides an amount that helps those working and earning a low income to reduce any taxes owed and can result in a tax refund.

Climate action incentive payment

Provides a quarterly payment for residents of Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba and Ontario to offset the cost of the federal pollution pricing.

GST/HST Credit

Provides four tax-free payments per year. If eligible, single individuals can get up to $496 per year. A couple with two children could get up to $992 per year.

Child disability benefit (CDB)

Provides up to $264 per month to families who care for a child under age 18 who has a severe impairment.

Canada Dental Benefit

Provides and interim benefit for eligible families with children under 12 who do not have access to a private dental insurance plan

You don’t have to apply for the benefits and credits every year. But every year you must:

  • File your income tax and benefit return on time every year, even if you have no income in the year. If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they also have to file a return every year.
  • Keep your personal information updated with the CRA

  • Keep your supporting documents in case the CRA asks for them as part of the validation process

Learn about Canadian taxes

Many of the benefits people enjoy in Canada are made possible through taxes. If you are an employee, your employer usually deducts taxes and pays them to the CRA for you.

Each year, people complete a tax return (Income Tax and Benefit Return) to find out if they owe tax or if they will get money back.

Understand how the Canadian tax system works: Learn about your taxes

Be aware of tax scams

Be cautious if you are contacted by someone that claims to be from the CRA and requests personal information such as your SIN, credit card number, bank account number, or passport number.

Scams may contact you by phone, email, mail or instant messaging.

We will never:

  • ask you to pay fees using prepaid credit cards, gift cards, or any other similar services
  • use aggressive language
  • threaten to harm, arrest, or deport you

Read more about scams and how to confirm if the CRA contacted you: Scam prevention and the CRA

Know your rights

You have 16 rights when dealing with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). These are known as the Taxpayer Bill of Rights. These rights build upon the CRA's corporate values of professionalism, respect, integrity, and collaboration.
