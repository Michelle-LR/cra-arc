Who can apply

The CRA will accept a request for an adjustment beyond the normal 3-year period to the income tax return of another individual if they meet specific eligibility criteria.

Eligibility criteria

You must meet all of the following to be eligible:

  • You have past due returns that are beyond the 3-years from either the:

    • End of the tax year to file an income tax return to claim a tax refund
    • Date of the original notice of assessment to request an adjustment to an assessment issued for a previous tax year

  • You are either:

    • An individual (other than a trust)
    • A graduated rate estate
      If you a testamentary trust that is not a graduated rate estate

      As of January 1, 2016, a testamentary trust (that is not a graduated rate estate) can no longer do the following for the 2016 and later tax years, after the normal three-year period has expired:

      • file an income tax return with the intent of getting a refund
      • request an adjustment to a previously filed return with the intent of reducing the tax payable

      Testamentary trusts can still file a return or ask for an adjustment for the 2015 and previous tax years.

  • You were not aware of, or missed, claiming a deduction or credit that is available for the year, including any overpayment of taxes deducted from your salary

  • None of the following apply to you:
    • Dispute an assessment or reassessment

      The purpose for requesting an adjustment beyond the normal three-year period can not be to dispute how correct or valid a previous assessment is.

      The CRA will not allow an adjustment beyond the normal three-year period when the individual or graduated rate estate chose not to dispute the issues through the normal objection or appeal process. For example, an audit reassessment, or where the issues were already dealt with under the objection process. For more information, go to File an objection.

    • Requests affecting the statute-barred income tax return of another individual

      The CRA will generally not accept a request for an adjustment beyond the normal three-year period to the income tax return of another individual. For example, if the adjustment would result in the increase of one of the following:

      • taxes
      • interest

      The CRA cannot reassess penalties on the income tax return of another individual when the time limit for objecting to, or requesting relief from, has already passed (statute-barred).

    • Provincial benefits or credits

      The legislation that allows the CRA the discretion to refund or reduce the amount payable beyond the normal three-year period does not override provincial limitations, unless it is indicated in the provincial act. For example, if there are time limits to claim certain provincial benefits or credits (that the CRA administers on behalf of the provinces).

    • Permissive deductions

      The CRA will not process requests for adjustments when the taxpayer originally claimed less than the maximum amount allowed. For example, if the requested decrease in tax is the result of an increased claim for capital cost allowance or other allowable deductions. For more information, see Information Circular IC84-1, Revision of Capital Cost Allowance Claims and Other Permissive Deductions.

    • Employment Insurance premiums and Canada Pension Plan contributions

      The legislation does not allow the minister to issue refunds beyond the normal three-year period for:

      • Employment Insurance (EI) premiums
      • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions

      Exceptions

      • The time limit for a refund of an overpayment for EI premiums is three years while CPP contributions is four years.
      • In the case of retroactive Canada Pension Plan Disability pension benefits, effective September 1, 2010, the time limit for a refund of CPP overpayments is 10 years.
  • Requests based on a court decision or other resolution

    If the request is made as a result of a court decision, the CRA will generally not allow for the reassessment of a return beyond the normal three-year period. Requests made to reassess a return beyond the normal three-year period based only on the successful appeal by another taxpayer will not be granted. For more information, see Information Circular IC75-7R3, Reassessment of a Return of Income.

    Similarly, knowledge of another taxpayer's negotiated settlement to resolve an objection, or another taxpayer's consent to judgment on an appeal, will generally not be allowed an adjustment beyond the normal three-year period if the taxpayer has chosen not to protect his or her right of objection or appeal.

  • Invalid requests

    There are certain requests that cannot be considered for relief under the Taxpayer Relief Provisions. For more information, go to Invalid requests.

  • Late, amended, or revoked elections

    We cannot consider a request to accept late, amended, or revoked elections:

    • for a tax year that ended outside of the 10 previous calendar years
    • that are not listed in the List of prescribed elections or designations and allocations that are Deemed Prescribed Elections.
  • Requests for relief or adjustments beyond a 10-year period

    Certain requests for relief or adjustments are limited to within a 10-year period. For more information, go to Limitation period on exercising discretion and the deadline for requesting relief.
